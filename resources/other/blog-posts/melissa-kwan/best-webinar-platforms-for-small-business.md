Small businesses face unique considerations when choosing a webinar platform. They should look for a solution that:
Is easy to use
— You shouldn’t need extensive training just to get up and running.
Helps you scale without adding headcount
— Small teams need to do more with less. The right platform lets you run webinars at scale (more sessions, more topics, more attendees) without hiring additional team members or burning out your current ones.
Maintains engagement and personalization at scale
— Scaling shouldn’t mean sacrificing the attendee experience. Look for features that keep webinars interactive and relevant, even when you’re running hundreds of sessions per month.
Supports long-term growth
— Choose a flexible platform that adapts to evolving strategies, audiences, and goals so you don’t have to switch systems later.
You should also consider your use case — why are you interested in webinar software, and what types of webinars will you run?
There are
two main types of webinar platforms
:
live
(for real-time events) and
automated
(for pre-recorded webinars that run on autopilot).
Live
is best when you need real-time feedback that shapes the webinar itself — like internal alignment meetings, one-time special events, AMAs, town halls, or virtual conferences with panel discussions.
Automated
is best when you're delivering the same content repeatedly — like
onboarding new customers
,
training sessions
,
educational courses
, or
product demos
. Automation lets small teams scale their reach dramatically without hosting webinars nonstop: some eWebinar customers with just one or two team members run hundreds of hours of webinars per month across nearly 100 different topics.
A common misconception about automated webinars is that they're "just recordings." In reality, automated webinar platforms include robust engagement features — live chat, polls, Q&A, CTAs, and more — so you can design interactive experiences that keep viewers engaged. 4. Demio
Demio
is another straightforward, browser-based webinar platform that supports both live and automated webinars.
This solution is most appealing to sales teams and marketers looking to drive lead generation through webinars because it offers built-in marketing tools, dozens of integrations, and detailed analytics.
The analytics let you see exactly who attended, how long they stayed, when they dropped off, which resources they downloaded, and how they answered polls — so you can better understand audiences and identify wins and losses. Demio makes it easy to build and run webinars, whether you’re hosting live or setting webinars to play on autopilot. It comes with some nice engagement features, too — Q&As, polls, CTAs, offers, links, and chat. Live sessions also support attendee participation, so hosts can bring attendees “to the stage” and let them ask questions or join discussions.
Another standout benefit here is the mobile experience. Users can join webinars on the go, tuning in from their cell phones or tablets, and Demio focuses attendees on one thing at a time instead of cramming everything onto a tiny screen.

Those watching on demand can’t get in touch with your team unless they email you separately (and who’s going to do that?), so on-demand viewing is a pretty passive experience for attendees.
And not to sound like a broken record, but pricing could also be an issue here, as Demio is a little expensive for small teams. Most teams need the Growth plan, costing between $117 to $855 per host per month, and that plan only supports 3k seats.

10. WebinarJam
Last on our list is
WebinarJam
— we include them because they’re a long-standing industry name that small businesses are likely to come across when comparing webinar platforms, but they’re far from a top recommendation because the platform supports some sketchy, disingenuous practices.
WebinarJam is designed for sales teams, course creators, coaches, and solopreneurs running online selling events — both live and automated. It offers standard engagement tools like chat, polls, and Q&A, but the real focus is on driving immediate conversions through offers, countdown timers, and social proof features that show how many people are attending and purchasing. The whole vibe is infomercial-style urgency: buy now before seats run out or the special offer expires.
The problem here is that WebinarJam shows fake engagement throughout webinar experiences. They show bloated attendee counts, fake chats or likes, and fake purchases to create the illusion that other interested buyers are attending. This can light a fire for other attendees to “Act now!” before it’s too late.
The other fake-out here is that automated webinars display as LIVE, so attendees always think they’re attending a real-time sales event.
All of this is really dishonest, and it’s not the way to get customers (or retain loyal ones). And most of the time, attendees catch on to this scam. They get the idea that something isn’t right and leave the webinar, with total distrust in your team. At that point, you kill webinar attendance and engagement rates, and lose those potential customers for good. This is especially harmful to small businesses trying to build and keep a customer base.
