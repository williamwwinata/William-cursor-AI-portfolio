How do webinars generate leads?
Webinars generate leads by providing educational content that's valuable to a specific target audience. This allows you to build a database of prospects with their names and email addresses while simultaneously providing an opportunity to promote your product, service, or expertise during the webinar.
Benefits of webinar lead generation
While the obvious benefit is your ability to generate high-quality leads, there's more nuance to the return on investment that comes from hosting lead-generation webinars. Let's take a brief look at just a few of the benefits that you stand to gain:
Bulk lead qualification
Industry credibility
Time/money saved
Bulk lead qualification
Instead of manually qualifying individual leads, webinars allow you to qualify prospects en masse. In fact, it's more like prospects
qualifying themselves
since they'll be able to discern whether the product or service is right for them while they're watching the webinar.
If your offer doesn't appeal to them then they'll likely disqualify themselves as a lead before you expend any energy trying to close them. This ensures that only qualified leads make their way to the bottom of your sales funnel and saves you the time you would've otherwise wasted on mismatched prospects.
Another consideration is the fact that you'll often be dealing with hot leads when hosting a webinar since cold prospects wouldn't have registered unless they were already interested in some way. This influx of warm traffic increases the odds that you'll get a worthwhile number of conversions from your webinar.
Industry credibility
Aside from the raw revenue you'll generate from closing deals, hosting webinars also helps you establish credibility and come across as an expert in your space. This could help you land larger clients down the line who are looking for industry veterans with a solid track record.
You'll also boost your word-of-mouth marketing because more leads will be exposed to your content and start talking about you within their social circles. This makes it possible to leverage other people's audiences which can massively increase your reach.
You can even hand out additional resources during your webinar that further display your expertise and address specific pain points that your customers often face. These downloadable resources serve not only as a value-add to attendees but a viral lead magnet to anyone else they share it with.
Time/money saved
By generating inbound leads instead of prospecting through outbound marketing methods, you'll save a ton of outreach time. It's also easier to generate interest in a webinar than it is to spend hours on cold pitching your products and services directly only to get rejected or ignored.
Beyond time savings, you'll also save a lot of money by relying on organic content like webinars for lead generation instead of resorting to more expensive methods like paid ads. After all, you'd otherwise be paying around $2 for every click on Facebook ads and as much as $7 or more for each click on LinkedIn.
This may not sound like a lot at first but, once you try to scale the reach of your ad campaigns, you'll quickly find that it's far less sustainable than webinar lead generation. This is especially true for startups and SMBs that don't have a fully funded war chest reserved for their marketing and advertising efforts.

1. Automate your webinars
Once you’ve hosted webinars that reliably generate quality leads, you can scale your efforts by recording the presentation and turning it into an automated webinar. This decouples the number of leads you generate from the time you spend hosting webinars.
After all, you’ll only have to record the webinar once and can then have thousands of registrants attend it in perpetuity. In other words, webinar automation serves as a force multiplier that increases lead volume while decreasing the time investment required.
As long as you have this type of
automated webinar funnel
, all you'll need to do is keep pushing traffic to the top of the funnel and watch revenue come out the bottom. Instead of dedicating all your time to hosting, you can re-focus your energy on
promoting your webinar
on new channels.
This strategy is especially effective for the types of webinar presentations that you host over and over again, such as product/sales demos and customer/employee onboarding. You’re mostly reciting the same script ad infinitum so why wouldn’t you automate it?

4. Make webinars interactive
Whether you're hosting webinars live or automating them, you should always strive to make the event as interactive as possible. Interactive webinars keep attendees engaged for longer which increases watch time as a result.
The thing is, it's a lot easier to make webinars interactive when you're recording them in advance rather than presenting live. Even as early as the planning stage, you'll have the unique opportunity to coordinate your interactive elements ahead of time so that they fit seamlessly into your webinar content.
For instance, you could write your webinar script in such a way that it organically segues into the polls you'll display. And it's not just polls! You could run quizzes, ask questions, or display helpful tips to enrich the attendee experience.
