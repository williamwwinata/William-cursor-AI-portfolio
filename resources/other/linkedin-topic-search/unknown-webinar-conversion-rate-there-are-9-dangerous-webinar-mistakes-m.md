There are 9 dangerous webinar mistakes most experts and coaches make and how to fix each one.


1. AUDIENCE TEMPERATURE → The right question to ask is; Were the people on your webinar warm or cold audience?


Cold audience are those from paid ads to strangers, registered people who don't know you, those whom it is their first time hearing about you and when you notice low engagement during webinar.


Warm audience are those who already follow you, have consumed your content, engage with your posts and ask questions during webinar.


Cold audience webinar conversion rate is usually 1-3% in most cases and warm audience webinar conversion rate 10-20% in most cases, so if you had launched a webinar to cold traffic, that's your problem.


2. TOPIC POSITIONING → Did your webinar title speak to a very deep pain point or just a surface-level topic? I have realized that weak titles don’t convert.


Your webinar title should identify a specific problem your ideal client has, promise a specific outcome and create curiosity about the "how".


3. WEBINAR STRUCTURE → Did you teach or did you sell?


A successful webinar is 50% teaching and 50% selling and if otherwise just prepare a free training and make it clear from the onset. If you're teaching for more than 45 minutes on a 60-minute webinar, you're teaching too much.


4. THE OFFER → Was your offer clear, compelling, and connected to their pain?


Your offer presentation should recap the problem and your solution, present your offer as the vehicle to implement the solution, focus on outcomes, not features, create legitimate urgency and finally have a clear call-to-action.


5. OBJECTION HANDLING → Did you address objections before they came up?


If you didn't address these objections during the webinar, most people will leave with doubts and doubts kills conversion.


6. SOCIAL PROOF → Did you show proof that your method works?


After explaining the problem, show someone who had that problem, after teaching the framework show someone who used it and during the offer presentation show transformations.

9. FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCE → The Question: Did you follow up with people who attended but didn't buy?


Webinars still work but only if you launch to a warm audience, you position the topic correctly addressing pain points, you balance teaching with selling, you present your offer compellingly, you handle objections, you create urgency and you follow up strategically.


Don't give up on webinars, instead fix the gaps and launch better.
