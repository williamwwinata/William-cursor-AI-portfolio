A guy in Jeremy’s Inner Circle went from $10K/month in ad spend to $10K/day

No in-between

That's the most audacious risk I've ever seen someone take

Here's what happened:

He was doing $200-300K/month organically in crypto research for 4 years

Joined Jeremy’s Inner Circle, watched the closer math video and used the financial model spreadsheet for webinars

Did the math on his webinar funnel

I told him on our one-on-one: "What the fuck are you doing? Why aren't you just ripping it since you’re so profitable right now?"

He spent $300K on ads that month

Went straight to $1M/month

Do you have any chance in hell to pull off something similar? Hell no, you’re odds of ever doing this are likely sub .000000001% and you aren;t a special snowflake, you are likely a dumb ass with no chance of turning money into more money. 

Back to the story -

The scariest part?

He had ONE closer handling the entire thing

No setter, no backup

That guy closed $1M cash collected in his first month - booked solid 7am to 7pm for 2 weeks straight with zero breaks

He said it felt like "putting $300K on roulette, leaving for a week, and coming back to see if it landed on black"

But here's the thing about that bet - he had the math down

He knew his cost per call, conversion rates, and average deal size

The numbers worked, he just needed the balls to pull the trigger

Three $1M months in a row followed

Then fulfillment broke - he wasn't prepared for the delivery side with 18 team members

The cash helped him fix problems faster, but he admits he probably spent it on random shit instead of the right places early on

Now he's got a new problem:

He got his million-dollar month trophy and the feeling lasted about 17 seconds

New money, new problems, new responsibility

But his girlfriend quit her job, his team is dialed, and he's obsessed with building the Morningstar of crypto

His new target? The $5M/month trophy

Because going from $300K to $1M was a 5x

And going from $1M to $5M is just another 5x

Same multiple, bigger number

That's how you have to think at this level
