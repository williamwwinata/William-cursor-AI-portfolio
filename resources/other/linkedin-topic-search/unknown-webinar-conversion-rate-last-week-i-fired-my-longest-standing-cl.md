Last week, I fired my longest-standing client. Actually, It was hard and an emotional one.😭

In 2023, just a few months into my media buying career, he found through a Facebook post.

We jumped on a call.
His Meta ads were bleeding.
I told him things we should do differently.

I got into the Meta ads account, ran experiments, made strategic adjustments, and results started coming in. Now, work has fully started with him.

His model was simple:
Free webinars → paid program offer.

Within 4 months, we were generating consistent qualified leads. Then we scaled into YouTube Ads and a bit TikTok Ads. Previous learnings helped.

There were failures.
There were big wins.
Wins that made it very hard to walk away now last week.😅

Here’s what we built together about 3 years:

• 124,000+ qualified webinar leads
• 10–15% average webinar conversion rate
• Up to 20% on strong YouTube campaigns
• ₦50M+ spent on Meta
• ₦16M+ spent on Google Ads

But here’s the interesting part.

When he came into my life, I was a junior media buyer with no structure. He was my main client, and I became fully available.

Midnight WhatsApp messages.
Landing page URL updates at 1am.
Daily monitoring.
High-spend days that required extreme attention.

For 3 years, I was on 24/7 mode.

I became so obsessed with his campaigns and business. Almost irreplaceable. He even offered to increase my pay and renegotiate work terms.

But growth sometimes requires distance.

I need structure around my work.
I need headspace, maybe a bit of work-life balance 
I need room to explore other industries and sharpen my craft at a higher level.

So I made the difficult decision to step away, and I have no regrets.

Sometimes the hardest move in your career isn’t chasing more.

It’s choosing alignment over attachment.

And noww..

On to the next chapter. 🚀
