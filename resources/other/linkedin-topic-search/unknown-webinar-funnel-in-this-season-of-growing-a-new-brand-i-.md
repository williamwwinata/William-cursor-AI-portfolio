In this season of growing a new brand, I'm reflecting on the lessons I've learned building Your ID and other offerings. Let's start with a few things to know about marketing via webinars...

Top of funnel visibility is the HARDEST part in my opinion. You need an audience to see your events before they can even be compelled to join. When you're starting from scratch, this means you're working with ads, freebies, influencers, in-person networking, etc. to gain traction. 

Setting up registration is quick. Setting up the right landing pages, follow-up campaigns and automations is what takes time.

Your registration rate is NOT your show-up rate, especially when the event is free. Typically you're working with about 10-20% showing up live for a free workshop (results may vary). Another 1-3% will watch the replay.

It can feel like if you're only talking to 10 people, it doesn't matter. Why put much energy into wowing them? But you better!

Those people are your people, and they're giving you their time and energy. Even if just one person shows up, you better have something of value to give them because your job is to serve your audience. Your job is to build genuine brand community. And hopefully, those people see that you do offer value and in time become brand ambassadors.

Now if you're doing sales on the webinar, it's a whole extra later of the numbers game. From the people who join, you're working with a 1-3% conversion rate.

This all can feel very overwhelming when you're starting from scratch with tiny audiences. Smart campaigns and in-person networking can help you grow faster, but mostly, it's a consistency play. Keep after it, do a good job, and with time, you'll build a community of people who you can genuinely help and who appreciate you for what you bring to the table.
