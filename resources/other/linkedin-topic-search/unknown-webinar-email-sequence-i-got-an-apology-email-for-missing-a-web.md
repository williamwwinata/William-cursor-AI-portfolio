I got an apology email for missing a webinar last week.

From a team that never even tried to get me there.

"We're sorry you couldn't attend."

I actually laughed out loud.

You're sorry? Okay... but sorry doesn't explain what went wrong here.

Out of curiosity, I went and checked my spam folder.

Guess what?

2 reminder emails - all sitting there, never made it to me.

So think about what that actually means.

No reminders means no anticipation.
No anticipation means no reason to show up.
No show-ups means empty seats at a webinar they,
probably spent weeks putting together.

And the fix they landed on?

Apologize and bury a product pitch at the bottom of a replay email.

I felt nothing... genuinely nothing.

Now if I felt nothing,
someone who actually wanted to attend,
imagine what everyone else on that list felt.

That's where the money actually went.

Not just one missed attendee.

It's a room full of people who registered with the intention.

And just quietly drifted off into nothing.

Because the registration confirmation was the last time anyone actually thought about them.

I couldn't stop thinking about it after I closed that email.

So I sat down and mapped out exactly how I would've handled it differently at every stage.

If you want to see it, it's in the carousel below.
