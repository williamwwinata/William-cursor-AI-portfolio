"So...$900 for this landing page?"

I recently had an interesting exchange with one of clients. 

I handed him a full webinar registration optin page - the copy, the framework, and a few other secrets.

And although this work is based off perhaps one of the biggest and most influential brands in the space (and I know for a FACT this structure works because I've used it for other clients to generate great results, and after getting  a head nod from my mentors)...

My client looked at what I handed him and was a bit skeptical because it just looked...too short. 

So when I quoted $900 for this simple but high-performing lander...I immediately got some pushback.

(Don't worry, I ended up quoting the full amount after explaining to him why I quoted this number 👇 )

See, my client looked at the finished product in terms of how many words it had:  the headline, some authority-cementing eyebrow copy, a few bullets...

All adding up to 192 words.

But if you've ever worked with a marketer / copywriter before...

Then you know that for each finished product...

There's a WHOLE lot more that DOESN'T "make it" to the final version. 

For this lander it was:

12+ headline variations...

30+ bullets...

And 2 FULL landers I previously wrote with a completely different angle each. 

And this is why I'm SO against charging "per word" like many positions here on this platform tend to favor...

Because in creative work like copywriting and marketing...

There's often MORE that doesn't make it to the final version...than what the finished product shows. 

Same with "hourly" rates for this line of work (I'll get to this on another post...but it really follows a similar train of thought).

Okay, Monday rant's over. Back to work.
