🔥 Evergreen funnels or webinars — what’s the smartest way for course creators to scale?

On one side, webinars deliver live (or automated) events packed with value, creating a surge of trust and excitement that moves people to buy right now. They let you shift from selling 1:1 to 1-to-many, leveraging your time by speaking to dozens or hundreds at once instead of repeating the same pitch on endless sales calls. 

This is especially powerful for selling high-ticket programs because you can handle objections live and build an emotional connection fast.

But webinars can also be energy-draining. Hosting live sessions over and over eats up your time, and even automated webinars need constant updates to stay relevant. If you don’t keep improving them, conversions can drop off quickly.

On the other side, evergreen funnels are like a quiet money machine. They work around the clock, generating sales without your daily involvement. They shine with proven offers, letting you create predictable revenue while you focus on serving clients, improving products, or taking time off.

Yet evergreen funnels can also feel impersonal if you don’t nail your messaging and follow-up sequences. Without a sense of urgency, leads might procrastinate and never buy.

The truth? Both strategies can generate massive income — when matched with the right offer, audience, and execution.

Evergreen funnels can bring consistent cash flow. Webinars can deliver explosive spikes of revenue. Smart course creators often combine both: use webinars for live energy and quick revenue boosts, and evergreen funnels to stabilize income long-term.
