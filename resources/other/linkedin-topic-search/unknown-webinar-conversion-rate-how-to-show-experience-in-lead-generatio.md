How to show experience in lead generation and closing deals
Common mistakes recruiters notice
• 	Candidates say “I generated leads” or “I closed deals” without numbers or context
• 	Listing responsibilities instead of measurable achievements
• 	Failing to distinguish between lead generation (finding prospects) and deal closing (converting them)
• 	Overlooking tools and strategies used (CRM, cold calling, digital campaigns, referrals)
Best practices recruiters expect
• 	Quantify lead generation: “Generated 200 qualified leads per quarter through LinkedIn outreach and webinars.”
• 	Show conversion rates: “Converted 30% of leads into paying clients, exceeding industry average by 10%.”
• 	Highlight deal size and value: “Closed 15 enterprise contracts worth $1.5M annually.”
• 	Connect to business impact: “Improved pipeline efficiency, reducing sales cycle length by 20%.”
• 	Demonstrate strategy: mention how you sourced leads (networking, inbound marketing, referrals) and how you nurtured them to closure
• 	Balance both sides: recruiters want to see you can generate opportunities and convert them into revenue
Sample recruiter-ready phrasing
• 	“Generated 300 qualified leads via targeted email campaigns, closing 25% into new accounts worth $750K.”
• 	“Built a pipeline of 50 enterprise prospects, successfully closing 12 deals and achieving 130% of quota.”
• 	“Leveraged CRM analytics to prioritize high-value leads, increasing closing ratio from 20% to 35%.”
Recruiter insight
Recruiters confirm that the strongest candidates separate lead generation from deal closing, quantify both, and connect them to revenue growth. This proves you can manage the entire sales cycle, not just one stage.
