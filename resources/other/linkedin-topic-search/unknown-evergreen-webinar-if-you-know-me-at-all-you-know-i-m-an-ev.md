If you know me at all, you know I’m an evergreen queen.

I’ll preach to anyone who’ll listen that you need an automated funnel selling your membership on autopilot . And if you’ve ever worked with me privately, you know this is my hill to die on.

Because without an evergreen funnel, you’re making your buyer do way too much work.

BUT, and here’s the truth most people don’t want to hear ... relying on your automated system to do all the work?

That’s the fastest way to get stuck wondering, “Why isn’t this selling?”

 Evergreen funnels aren’t designed to save your business. They’re designed to stabilize it.

Yes, evergreen webinars, sales pages, and nurture sequences are brilliant. But on their own, they rarely turn a cold audience into loyal buyers at scale.

Why? Because your buyers aren’t one-size-fits-all:
👉 Some buy fast - they need a clear offer and a nudge.
👉 Some need to feel your energy - they’ll say yes only after a live connection.
👉 Some lurk for months - they need ongoing trust-building until they’re ready.

That’s why the most resilient memberships don’t choose either live launches or evergreen.

They layer them.

Evergreen gives you predictable, low-maintenance sales in the background.

Live touchpoints (webinars, workshops, bootcamps) keep your audience engaged and move the fence-sitters over the line.

And a steady flow of new leads keeps the whole system healthy.

So if your evergreen funnel feels flat, don’t scrap it.

Fix your layers first...

I break all of this down, plus the #1 reason most funnels fail 
and what to do instead in this article.
 
This hybrid approach isn’t flashy.

But it works. Every time.
