Over the last few years, I have strategised and executed a little over 250 webinars.

Two ideas have stayed with me:  
1) Watch for early signs of saturation  
2) Keep optimising the funnel, not just adding more webinars. 

In my own IELTS business, “webinar fatigue” showed up over time. Attendance moved from 𝟮𝟱𝟬–𝟯𝟬𝟬 𝗽𝗲𝗼𝗽𝗹𝗲 𝘁𝗼 𝗮𝗯𝗼𝘂𝘁 𝟭𝟬𝟬. End retention dropped from 𝟱𝟬–𝟲𝟬 𝗽𝗲𝗼𝗽𝗹𝗲 𝘁𝗼 𝟱–𝟲. For a while, 30–40% of revenue came from automated Everwebinar funnels. 

The automated webinar funnel helped briefly, but the same issue showed up there, too, because the content and structure were not keeping up with the audience. 

Later, during my stint at Simplilearn, my seniors nudged me to solve for this exact problem - webinar fatigue - and start optimising each stage of the webinar funnel. 

So we started fixing things one by one:

- 𝗪𝗵𝗼 the landing page brought in  
- 𝗛𝗼𝘄 the topic was framed  
- 𝗪𝗵𝗼 was speaking + 𝗵𝗼𝘄 they delivered  
- 𝗛𝗼𝘄 we followed up with attendees and non-attendees
