Last week a client rejected an entire pre and post webinar email sequence I worked on.

Just like that.

She said,  “This doesn’t sound like me.”
And I won’t lie, that one hurt a little.

Because this wasn’t random copy. We didn’t just sit and “write emails”.
Everything was based on voice of customer.

Customer interviews, her interview, Reddit, Quora, blogs.
Real words. Real phrases. Real problems.
Still, she felt it wasn’t her voice.

And this is where a lot of copywriters get defensive.
Start explaining. Start proving. Start protecting their work.
I didn’t.

I went back, sat with the other copywriter on the team, and we opened the emails again and went through them word by word.

Every claim, every line, every idea, we checked where it came from. Interview? Reddit? Her own words? Something a customer said?

We basically built a feedback loop around the emails so nothing was there just because it “sounded good”.

By the end of it, I felt this weird kind of satisfaction.

The kind you get when you know you didn’t just write something… you built it properly.

A lot of freelancers think this work is about writing.

A big part of this work is actually handling people’s fears around their own business, their voice, their audience, their reputation.

To you it’s a Google Doc.
To them it’s their business.

So when they push back, it’s rarely just about a sentence.

There’s usually hesitation, fear, uncertainty sitting behind that feedback.

Anyway, we worked through her objections, walked her through the thinking, and she decided to use those emails for her webinar in the end.

This job is rarely just writing.
Half the job is getting people comfortable with the words.
