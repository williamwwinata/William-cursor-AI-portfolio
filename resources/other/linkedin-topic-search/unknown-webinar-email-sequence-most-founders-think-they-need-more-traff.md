Most founders think they need more traffic.

They don’t.

They need fewer leaks.

I worked with a SaaS founder burning $18K/month on ads. Great product. Smart tech. Solid differentiation.

But her conversion rate? 0.7%.

She was convinced the problem was reach. More eyeballs. More content. More channels.

Nope.

The real issue was attention. Visitors weren’t staying because nothing told them where to look — or why it mattered.

We didn’t change her messaging. We stripped it.

Cut the features. Killed the jargon. Buried the “award-winning” fluff.

Left only the one thing her ideal customers actually cared about: the outcome.

We rewrote the homepage in plain English. One job. One audience. One message.

Then we redesigned the first five seconds of experience — not the whole funnel.

Result?

Conversion rate jumped to 4.1% in 21 days.

Same traffic. Same ad spend.

Revenue increased by $92K in six weeks.

No gimmicks. No webinars. No email sequences.

Just clarity, focus, and ruthless editing.

That’s the ATTENTION framework: stop shouting. Start guiding.

Most marketing fails because it tries to say too much to too many.

Winners win by narrowing.

By saying, “This is for you — and if it’s not, walk away.”

Attention isn’t about being seen.

It’s about being understood.

This is how we help founders move fast and sell without fluff.
