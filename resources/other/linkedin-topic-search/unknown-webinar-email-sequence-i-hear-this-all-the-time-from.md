I hear this all the time from new clients who are excited to scale. 

But here's the thing I tell them:

𝗬𝗼𝘂 𝗮𝗯𝘀𝗼𝗹𝘂𝘁𝗲𝗹𝘆 𝗻𝗲𝗲𝗱 𝘁𝗼 𝘃𝗮𝗹𝗶𝗱𝗮𝘁𝗲 𝗯𝗲𝗳𝗼𝗿𝗲 𝘆𝗼𝘂 𝗮𝘂𝘁𝗼𝗺𝗮𝘁𝗲.

Too many companies try to automate before they validate. They jump straight into automated LinkedIn outreach, evergreen webinars, or complex email sequences without knowing if their approach actually works.

Here's what validation looks like:

Test your campaign for 30-90 days (sometimes longer) to establish a baseline. You need to know that AD1 with this copy and graphic, EMAIL1 with this subject line and body, and LANDING PAGE1 with this headline actually convert.

Start with your lowest-hanging fruit - usually your email list. If you can't get your existing audience to engage, paid ads won't magically fix that.

I worked with a client who'd been running live webinars for months on the same winning topic. We knew the show-up rates, focus rates, and cost per registrant. Only then did we consider automation because we had real data to work from.

Once you validate and automate, then you can accelerate. Scale that winning campaign as far as it will go. Sometimes that means upgrading your team too - what got you here won't get you there.

The stages are simple: Validation. Automation. Acceleration.
