Simo Lemhandez recently told me they run two webinars every week at folk. He’s raised $9 million, runs a lean team, and is building so profitably he may never need to raise again — yet he still swears by webinars for business growth.

- Not for vanity.
- Not to look “thought-leadery.”

But because some users aren’t ready for a demo and he’s not letting them slip through the cracks.

Here’s what they’re doing that you’re NOT:

1. Webinars as mid-funnel magnets
Not everyone is purchase-ready. So instead of ghosting casual signups, they run live sessions to warm them up: twice a week, every week.

2. Zero-pressure education > Hard-pitch demos
The webinars aren’t selling.
They’re teaching.
When buyers understand, they convert. Simple.

3. Dynamic routing post-webinar
Attended a webinar and asked sharp questions? Pass to sales for quick follow-ups.
Just showed up and listened? They get nurtured through email flows and content.

4. Evergreen value
Each session becomes 5 pieces of content.
SEO juice, nurture campaigns, even ammo for outbound.

5. Product-led growth ≠ no sales
PLG doesn’t mean silent.
It means scalable. And webinars are the scalable version of hand-holding.

TAKEAWAY
Is your sales team wasting time chasing cold leads?
You probably don’t need more demos.
You need better education paths for your not-yet-ready users.

WANT TO KNOW SOMETHING CRAZY?
Even at Knorish, we’ve done webinars once a week every month for the last 2 years - and it’s been our go-to funnel for:
- Acquiring new customers
- Nurturing our existing audience
- And educating not-ready users into buying decisions

So next time a lead doesn’t book a call?
Give them a calendar invite to a webinar instead.
