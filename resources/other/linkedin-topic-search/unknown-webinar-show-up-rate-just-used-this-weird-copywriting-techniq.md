Just used this weird copywriting technique for a client and their webinar show-up rate more than doubled. 

It's called the "Damaging Admission" and it works like magic because it's the OPPOSITE of what most marketers do.

Instead of hiding flaws or limitations, you deliberately highlight them BEFORE your prospect finds them.

Examples:
"This system works, but it'll take at least 30 days to see results."
"Our approach gets great results, but only if you're willing to show up weekly."
"This isn't for everyone – if you need overnight success, we're not the right fit."

Why does this work? 

Because it triggers a psychological principle called the "paradox of honesty"; when someone admits a weakness, we automatically believe their claims about their strengths. 

I've helped clients add these strategic admissions to sales pages, emails, and webinar registrations with consistently strong results.
