One of the patterns I’ve seen repeatedly across startups, SMEs and smaller teams is that marketing rarely struggles because of effort. It struggles because of decision order.

Programmes come first. Purpose comes later. Then everyone ends up constantly adjusting execution instead of clarifying direction.
I’ve just published a long-form piece through The Big Brand Blueprint exploring this idea and the simple sequence that tends to work better in practice.

Purpose → Positioning → Principles → Programmes.

It's not a framework for adding more complexity, but a way to reduce it. Curious whether others recognise this pattern in their own organisations, read more...
