A marketing director asked me yesterday: "Why do our funnels feel so... broken?"

I didn't have an easy answer.

But that conversation stayed with me all night.

Because here's what I realized...

Most marketing funnels don't fail because of bad traffic or weak offers.

They fail because they're confusing.

We've built these elaborate digital journeys—landing pages that feed into email sequences that trigger Facebook pixels that retarget with three different ad variants that lead to webinar registration pages...

And somewhere in that complexity, we lost the customer.

The irony? 

We built complicated systems thinking they'd convert better.

But complexity isn't sophistication. 

It's just... complexity.

The best funnels I've seen share one trait:

They guide, they don't overwhelm.

They simplify, they don't complicate.

They convert, because they respect the customer's journey rather than hijack it.

This realization fundamentally changed how we approach funnel management.

Instead of adding more steps, we started asking: "What can we remove?"

Instead of optimizing every micro-conversion, we focused on clarity.

Instead of sophisticated automation, we prioritized simplicity.

The results? 

Fewer touchpoints. 

Higher conversions. 

Better customer experience.

Question for you: When's the last time you audited your marketing funnel for simplicity rather than sophistication?

What's one thing you could remove today that might actually improve your conversions?
