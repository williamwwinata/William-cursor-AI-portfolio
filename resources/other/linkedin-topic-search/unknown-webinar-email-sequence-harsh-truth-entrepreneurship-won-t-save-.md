Harsh truth:

Entrepreneurship won't save your stagnant dev career.

Most devs hit a plateau and immediately think "Maybe I should build a SaaS"

Their expectation is you build something once, ship it, and money rolls in forever.

You know how the reality looks like?

𝗧𝗿𝗮𝗳𝗳𝗶𝗰 𝗴𝗲𝗻𝗲𝗿𝗮𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻 = 60% of your time. Organic content / paid ads / outreach.
You're invisible without it.

𝗖𝗼𝗻𝘃𝗲𝗿𝘀𝗶𝗼𝗻𝘀 = another 30%. Sales calls / webinars / landing pages / email sequences.
You're now a marketer.

𝗗𝗲𝗹𝗶𝘃𝗲𝗿𝘆 = the rest. Customer support at 2 AM / bug fixes / retention strategies / pricing tests.
The actual product you love building is maybe 10% of the work.

And it takes 2+ years before you're profitable... If you make it.

𝗠𝗲𝗮𝗻𝘄𝗵𝗶𝗹𝗲 𝗮𝘀 𝗮 𝗱𝗲𝘃𝗲𝗹𝗼𝗽𝗲𝗿:
- Six figures in 2-3 years.
- Multi six figures as senior.
- Some hit $500k-1M with multiple companies.

You get paid every month. No 2 year runway needed.

Time freedom. Location freedom. Financial freedom.

No midnight customer emergencies unless you're on call rotation.

You should solve the problem in front of you first before thinking about the next one.
1. Stuck below $100k? Get there first.
2. Already senior? Push for $200k+ before you pivot.

Build your runway. Then chase entrepreneurship if you still want it.

Most realize they don't even want to leave once they hit that sweet spot.
