I can predict your next 6 months:

You bought a Webflow template. You'll hire a college kid to customize it. He'll disappear. You'll hire an agency to fix it. 

They'll quote $7000 to rebuild.

I've seen this 7 times.

Most B2B SaaS companies waste money on Webflow templates.

Not because templates are bad. Because they're cheap.

It starts with a simple idea: How can we save money and still have something good?

Finds a template. Everyone likes it. $50. Done.

3 weeks into the build, marketing team requests a popup on scroll depth.
Template doesn't have it.

"Nevermind. Can we have gated access for our playbook?"

Template doesn't have that either.

"Let's hire a Webflow developer. Shouldn't take more than a few hours, we can get it done for cheap."

They approach a Webflow certified agency. Quote: $2000. Seems absurd for "template edits."

They find a certified freelancer. Fully booked. Wedding next week. Sorry.

Finally, they find a college kid who agrees to do it for $200. "After all, it's just template editing and we're giving him exposure."

Founder cheered.

Two months later, the kid is gone. Turns out he joined a traditional company. 
As a marketeer. (He was a frontend dev who just finished a Webflow course.)

But the startup has scaled. They hired a CMO.

"Can we create a webinar registration page?"

No registration page in template.

"Can we build a comparison table? Us vs XYZ competitor?"

No comparison table in template.

So they go back to the agency. For 5 new landing pages.

Agency audits the build: 140+ custom classes. CMS is a mess. Static pages everywhere. No components. No structure.

Cannot fix it. Suggests rebuild.

The math:
Template: $50
College kid "edits": $200
Agency rebuild: $7000

Total: $7250

We could've built it properly from scratch for $5000 from day 1.

The cycle continues because "templates are cheaper."

They're not. They're just cheap upfront.

Stop rebuilding sites every 6 months. Stop wasting your CMO's time on website drama.

Let them focus on enterprise deals while we keep shipping pages that actually work.
