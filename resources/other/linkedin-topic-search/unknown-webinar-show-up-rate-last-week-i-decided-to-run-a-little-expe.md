Last week, I decided to run a little experiment.

I’d been doing webinars to sell my course—and for the first three webinars, I charged a tiny fee: just ₹99 to attend live.

The results blew me away.

✅ First webinar: 83% showed up.
✅ Second: 82%.
✅ Third: 93%.

And after 90 minutes of pouring my heart into the session, when I pitched my course—

🔥41% bought in the first webinar.
🔥35% in the second.
🔥33% in the third.

That’s not just good… it’s incredible.

But then, a thought crossed my mind.

"What if I remove the barrier completely? What if I do a free webinar?"

So, on 24th August, I gave it a shot.

And here’s what happened:

332 people registered.

Only 111 showed up.

Out of those, just 4 people bought.

That’s a heartbreaking 3% conversion. ☹️

It hit me like a brick. 💡

This was more than just numbers.

It was a lesson in MONEY PSYCHOLOGY.

See, when people pay—even a symbolic ₹99—they’re not just buying a ticket.

They’re buying commitment.
They’re saying, “I value my time, and I’ll show up.”

and when they show up, they are ready to upgrade their learning as well.

But when it’s free… it feels disposable.
People treat it like Netflix on their watchlist—“I’ll attend if I feel like it.”

The contrast is striking:
👉 Paid webinars: 80%+ show-up rate.
👉 Free webinar: 30% show-up rate.

And the difference in conversions? Worlds apart.

That day, I promised myself one thing:

No more free webinars.

Even if it’s just a small amount, always charge.
Because when people pay, they play.
And when they don’t… they just scroll.
