When someone lands on your seminar or webinar registration page, there is a short section that quietly determines whether they register or move on.

It’s the bullet points describing what they’ll discover by watching your presentation.

Most people treat these bullets as simple descriptions of the presentation.

That’s a mistake, because their job is not to summarize the presentation.

Their job is to make the right prospect think:

“I need to know this.”

And just as important, to signal to the wrong prospect that the presentation probably isn’t for them.

Over the years, I’ve found the strongest registration pages usually include three types of bullets.

First is the mistake bullet.

This highlights a risk the prospect may not realize they’re taking.

Example:

“The costly retirement mistake many high-income pre-retirees discover too late.”

Second is the discovery bullet.

This introduces something unfamiliar.

Example:

“A little-known strategy some high net worth retirees use to reduce taxes on their retirement income.”

Third is the outcome bullet.

This points toward a result they want.

Example:

“How smart Florida dentists structure their retirement income so market downturns have less impact.”

Some prospects register because they want to avoid mistakes.

Others because they’re curious about strategies they didn’t know existed.

And some because they want the result.

When your bullets include all three, more of the *right prospect*s decide to attend.

Here’s a simple test.

Look at the bullets on your registration page.

Do they merely describe the presentation?

Or do they create curiosity and relevance?

Can you make them more specific so they appeal to your ideal prospects?
