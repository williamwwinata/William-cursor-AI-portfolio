I’ve said it many times and I’ll say it again:

Do not blindly follow SEO tools’ advice.

Here’s an example: 

One tool keeps sending me alerts about orphan pages on my website. 

According to it, it’s an important issue that must be fixed as soon as possible. 

And potentially the tool could be right… if it were not wrong. 

First, what is an orphan page?

It’s a page on your website that doesn’t have any internal links. 

It kind of ‘cuts’ such a page from the website and makes it ‘unimportant’ from the organic search point of view. 
But what if it’s exactly what you want to achieve?

There are so many pages that can be orphan pages. For example, special landing pages, webinar registration pages, thank you pages, etc. 

And not having links to them is not an issue but a strategic decision. 

So here’s the thing:

SEO tools are great but they lack the website and business context that you have. So treat all their alerts as suggestions and learn to ignore those that don’t matter. 

Prioritizing the right fixes and ignoring the rest is an important part of your job as a technical SEO that requires deep knowledge.
