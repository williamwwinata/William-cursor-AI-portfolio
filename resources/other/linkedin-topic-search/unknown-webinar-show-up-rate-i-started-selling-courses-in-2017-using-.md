I started selling courses in 2017 using webinars
For the first 3 years, my webinars were free.

And honestly, I was proud of it.
I thought I was helping people, giving them value without asking for anything upfront.

Numbers looked great too:
40% showed up.
10% bought my course.

Life was good.
I felt like I had cracked the code.

Then came Reliance Jio.
Internet became dirt cheap.

Suddenly, the whole world was on social media.
And suddenly… my webinars?
Empty seats.

Show-up rate fell to 15%.
Conversions dropped to 3%.

I can’t explain how disheartening that felt.

You spend weeks preparing… pour your heart out for 90 minutes… and end up speaking to a screen where most people don’t even bother showing up.

I thought, let’s give it one last try.
But this time, no more freebies.

I only wanted people who genuinely cared to learn.
So I put a tiny entry fee: ₹99.

The results shocked me:
✅ 83% showed up. 41% bought.
✅ 82% showed up. 35% bought.
✅ 93% showed up. 33% bought.

I couldn’t believe it.
This wasn’t about money anymore.
It was about human psychology.
It was about commitment.

Free feels like “maybe.”
Paid feels like “I’ll be there.”

That day, I promised myself:
🚫 No more free webinars.

Because when people pay, they play.
When they don’t, they just scroll.

But here’s the tough part
Getting people to even pay ₹99 isn’t easy.

You need to design an offer so irresistible that people feel stupid saying no.

Otherwise, you’ll keep burning money on ads and wondering why no one buys.

This Saturday, I’m doing a session on exactly that:
“How to create offers people can’t refuse.”
