I was listening to a podcast with Amy Porterfield and Jenna Kutcher when Amy said something that stuck with me. We are in a trust recession.

Marketers everywhere are seeing lower engagement with launches, webinars, and events. Show-up rates are down and audiences seem more cautious. Maybe it's the pace of AI, the flood of content, or just the general state of things, but the shift is real.

As someone who works in content marketing at Ninety, I think about this a lot. How do we create content that doesn’t just inform but actually connects? That earns attention and keeps it?

I think the answer is getting back to the basics. More personal. More specific. More human.

If you're a marketer, creator, or founder, do you feel like we're in a trust recession?

What are you doing to build trust with your audience right now?
