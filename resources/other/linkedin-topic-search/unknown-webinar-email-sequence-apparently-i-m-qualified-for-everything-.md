Apparently, I’m qualified for everything… except being 25!

My interview started like this:

“WOW! Your experience is incredible!”

✔️ Video
✔️ Podcasts
✔️ Webinars
✔️ Email sequences
✔️ GTM strategy
✔️ Big energy
✔️ A whole career of wins

Then came the pivot.

“The role will be on camera a lot… the face of the company…”

Followed by that look…👀

 Not rejection.
Just calculation. Being observed and judged for my physical appearance.
A quiet, subtle, unmistakable:
“You’re not 25”

They didn’t say it out loud because ageism rarely speaks.

It just stares. And I stared right back because nowhere in the JD did it specify a certain look or age. But here we are. 

Truthfully?
You’re not gonna find a woman with the level of experience you require, who is 25.

What do you think? Should these forward-facing, camera-ready roles just include their specs up front?
