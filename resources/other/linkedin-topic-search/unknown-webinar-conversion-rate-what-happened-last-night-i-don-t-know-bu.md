“What happened last night?”
 “I don’t know, but I think I married a stripper.”
 — The Hangover

This is what Q2 feels like when a new CMO takes over and opens the dashboard.

MQLs are down. People get nervous. Someone suggests adding webinars.

But often, the drop isn’t failure, it’s sobriety.

Bad-fit leads stop counting. Volume-first programs get shut off. Standards go up. Vanity goes down.

Then something funny happens. Pipeline improves. Revenue follows. Sales stops complaining.

If you’re stuck in the MQL hangover phase, don’t panic.

Let volume fall. Track conversion. Reset expectations. 
Sometimes the hangover just means marketing finally grew up.
