I just published the Email Marketing Bible.

55,000 words. 16 chapters. 908 sources. 44 expert practitioners cited. Free and open source. Available as a Claude Code Skill or full text as PDF. 

I love email. I spent a decade in email marketing building SmartrMail (12K customers), studying what works across thousands of real accounts. Email is still the highest ROI channel in marketing and it's not even close.

And I love Claude. 

And I've automated a tonne of my GTM for Obooko with Claude. But email + AI is still clunky. 

It's 2 problems: the tools don't connect well to AI yet, and AI doesn't actually know email marketing nuance. Ask it to write a welcome sequence and you get generic, unsourced fluff. Ask about deliverability and it confidently makes things up.

I couldn't fix problem one in a week (ESPs not playing nicely with AI). but I could fix problem two by building a comprehensive knowledge base. 

I pulled every credible source I could find, cross referenced everything, cut the fluff. Reached out to 44 email experts across the internet. Scanned 908 sources and 4,798 extracted insights, then distilled it to 55,000 words across 16 chapters covering fundamentals, flows, deliverability, copywriting, compliance, 19 industry playbooks, platform selection, cold email, AI, and company case studies.

I reached out to 44 of the best email marketing practitioners in the world and got their feedback (thanks a million there folks). Then packaged the whole thing as a Claude Code AI skill. 

Install it and Claude becomes an email marketing co pilot that audits your setup, drafts sequences, pulls benchmarks for your industry, and gives sourced answers instead of hallucinations.

It's free and open source. It's incredibly helpful for the email marketing I'm doing, and I hope it's helpful for others.
