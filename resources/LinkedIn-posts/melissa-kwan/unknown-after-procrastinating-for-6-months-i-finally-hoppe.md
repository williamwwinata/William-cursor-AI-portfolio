After procrastinating for 6 months, I finally hopped on the newsletter bandwagon and launched "your founder next door", my bi-weekly column with relatable stories of what it’s like to build a company without an abundance of resources or friends in high places.

The first newsletter went out on Sunday, which included an intro and background about who I am and what I’m going to write about, so you can decide if it’s something compelling enough for you to stick around for. (Hopefully, the answer is, "YES!") If you're curious to read it, click on "Subscribe to Newsletter" under my name above.

🤔 Why "founder next door"? When I started sharing daily on social media, I asked myself how I could possibly differentiate myself from the sea of people who were way smarter and more experienced than me. Most people seemed like business demigods and I was nowhere near that ascent.

I decided then that I was going to be just me: your average, every day, relatable founder – the founder next door.  

As I shared more about my experineces on here, I noticed my most viral posts were ones that offered a window into the reality of what it’s like to build a startup with very few resources (not the “I’m always killing it” narrative). Overwhelmingly, the feedback I’ve gotten from readers on these posts have been about how refreshingly authentic it is to hear from a non-unicorn founder.

I didn’t make 30 Under 30, or even 40 Under 40. I didn’t go to Harvard. I’m just your founder next door who’s figuring it out as I go.

There’s so much BS out there, people claiming to know the secret to building a billion dollar company 🦄…foolproof playbooks that get you to $10m ARR so you can sell for $100m and go sip mojitos on a yacht in Ibiza. That content is not relevant to the majority of entrepreneurs in this world. Only 0.4% of SaaS businesses reach $10m ARR, and 0.001% make it to $50m ARR. On top of that, there’s simply no equation to success, just a bunch of loose algorithms.

My goal is to give you a glimpse into what it’s really like to build a company without an abundance of resources or friends in high places…because odds are that’s what you’re doing too.

With me, you’ll get relatable stories and personal experiences that will reassure you that you’re exactly where you need to be. As opposed to feeling completely inadequate for not being featured on Techcrunch.

‘your founder next door’ is my way of speaking directly to people like you who enjoy my writing and hopefully can benefit from my bootstrapping successes and failures.

💌 P.S. Everything I write on "your founder next door" will not be published in its entirety anywhere else.

If this sounds interesting for you, subscribe and see for yourself. You can unsubscribe anytime - no hard feelings!
