Don’t confuse media validation with customer validation. 📰 

Just because nobody wants to write about you, doesn't mean you're NOT onto something.

The media and your customers are motivated by opposite things. What the media wants to talk about is clickbait, something unique and groundbreaking.

Sometimes the execution of that is too hard for consumers to understand.

What your customers need is something that solves a problem and something simple they get right away.

Sometimes that can be pretty uninteresting.

In my last startup, our v1 was an homebuying app that pair with beacons (near field hardware) which sent info to people's phones when they walked into a house. Everyone wanted to write about it - but it never got adoption. Remember beacons? You shouldn't. It was too bleeding edge and never took off.

When we stripped every feature except making that app an "open house sign-in form", getting homebuyers to sign-in using an iPad instead of pen and paper, the business took off. But, nobody would write about it because there was nothing special about a check-in app.

💰 There's only one thing that matters in business: Revenue > Cost
