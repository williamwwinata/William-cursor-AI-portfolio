Before eWebinar, I had no marketing experience, which turned out to be my biggest shortcoming in growing this company. Nobody on our team did either, so we did the best we could, talked to anyone who was willing to help, and educated ourselves on the internet.

I spent my career in enterprise sales before becoming an entrepreneur. My first two companies sold products to big companies, which I grew through sheer will, cold calling, and lobby-conning (the subtle art of stalking the lobbies of conferences). I was a sales-led founder.

eWebinar was different. With a starting price point of $99/month, it needed to be a product-led, customer-led, and marketing-led company. While founder-led sales got us off the ground, it became unsustainable after 9 months when my network and sanity exhausted.

We threw everything against the wall to see what stuck and brute forced our way to $1m ARR, 36 months after product launch. On our way there, we tried a bunch of go-to-market strategies that DID NOT WORK. ☹️

On Kyle Poyar's Growth Unhinged today, I shared in detail about the top 10 go-to-market strategies that got us virtually zero ROI (why we did it and why it failed), including one strategy that we spent $130k and 10 months on. 💸💸

In the early days, our business had no credibility, product had little adoption, and we hadn’t built brand awareness. Things we thought would work, that worked for others, didn't move the needle for us at all.

In hindsight, a lot of the lessons learned should have been obvious. But we didn’t have the experience and expertise to know – and that’s part of the process of growing a startup, figuring out what works and what doesn’t.

💡 Reality is, the effectiveness of go-to-market strategies depends on many things: product, stage, messaging, time, investment, execution…what works as the business matures and changes may not work for a new startup, and vice versa.
