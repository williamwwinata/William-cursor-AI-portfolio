I was on a podcast interview with Alex a few weeks ago where he asked me, "Where do you see eWebinar in 5 years?" 🤔 

"I can't see myself doing this for more than 2 years." I replied. "I've been in tech for 14 years, I'm tired. I want to dabble in other industries that have more instant gratification. I want to create and build something I can see and hold (like in real estate or consumer goods)."

Alex pushed back on my response and said, "What if you can have both? Here's how I think about my business..."

Everything he told me that day is embodied in this post, one of my favorites he's ever written.

👉 The gist of it was:
• Significantly lower burn even at the cost of slower growth, because long term, it doesn't matter.
• Focus on profits and pay ourselves well enough to start side projects, don't wait if it brings joy.
• Hire founder replacements when there's enough profit, everybody wins.

I gotta admit, I haven't been having fun for the last 1.5 years. I just spent the last few months untangling what I call "my first mid-life crisis".  I'm writing a piece on this...His philosophy really got me thinking about my own expectations.

What Alex is saying here, is what all bootstrapped founders need to hear.

💡 There isn't a right or wrong way to build a business, but there is a right way FOR YOU.

We spend all of our time and energy chasing revenue, chasing growth rate, chasing potential multiples; there is nothing in the world more valuable than freedom.

We think we want to be rich, but we just want to be free.

If our startup is simply a gateway to the freedom we seek, what if we can have that today, instead of 5 years from now?

What if all it takes is changing the way we operate to make our business work for us?
