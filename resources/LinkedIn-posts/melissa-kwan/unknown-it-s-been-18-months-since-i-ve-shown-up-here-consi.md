It’s been 18 months since I’ve shown up here consistently. I'm finally ready to tell you why 👇 Not because I didn’t want to, but because I couldn’t.

After a gradual decline, I hit peak burnout a year ago, the thing I never thought could happen to me. Where I come from, burnout wasn’t real. I saw it as weakness and poor work ethic.

When I started losing motivation, I thought I just needed a break after being in the game for 14 years.

For months, I took it easy and convinced myself it was my reward for hard work. Every time I tried to restart the engine, I couldn't find the energy.

Eventually, I stopped showing up. Not just for work, but for myself and my most important relationships. From the outside, it looked like I was living my best life. In truth, I was distracting myself from the responsibilities and work I could no longer bring myself to do.

I lost trust in myself and questioned whether I was capable of running this company, whether I was being greedy for wanting more, whether I should just be content. I shrank my dreams because I shrunk myself. What we build is a reflection of who we are; when I plateaued, so did my company.

All this drove me to my first self-development retreat, Hoffman, a life-changing experience which I shared in my newsletter. There, I learned my "laziness" was actually exhaustion. Not the kind you feel after a marathon, but the kind your soul feels from 30+ years of believing you're not good enough to have what you most desire.

That clarity marked the start of my healing. It forced me to ask the most important question I’ve ever asked: Why don’t you love yourself? 💔 

I thought about selling my company, but something didn’t sit right. I believe too deeply in what we’ve built at eWebinar. It's a good business with potential to be a great one, and we've barely scratched the surface. It would be irresponsible to walk away before showing the world what’s possible with it.

The inner work brought me back to the version of myself before the programming, before the failure and pain; the 5-year old in the back of a taxi who stared at skyscrapers thinking, "One day, I'm going to build one of those." Because she knew she could.

🪄  I'm sharing this to let you know: I’m back. But not as the same person who left. Now, I have more confidence, trust, and peace than ever before. If our companies are reflections of us, when we heal, they grow.

I'm ready to build again, write again, and share again. 💪 

This time, not as a founder on a startup journey, but as a startup founder on a human journey.

I'm not here to share business strategies as I can't pretend to know more than experts we follow.

I'm here to share stories about the life that happens in pursuit of freedom. 🕊️ 

Find me here 4x a week, on Instagram daily, and bi-weekly on Substack.

PS. There will be zero AI content. 😂
