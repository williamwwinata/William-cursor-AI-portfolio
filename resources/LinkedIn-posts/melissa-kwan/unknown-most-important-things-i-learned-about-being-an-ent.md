Most important things I learned about being an entrepreneur in the last 15 years: 👩‍🏫 

1. Try before you buy.
Don't split equity before you know if you work well together, most people don't. Be a contractor, charge for your experience & output, not by the hour. You work fast because you are good.

2. Value yourself or no one else will.
Charge what you believe you are worth, deliver above expectations. Your value cannot be determined by someone's inability to see your worth; or worse, someone's inability to pay.

3. You pay peanuts, you get monkeys.
The worst customers are the ones who pay the least. The opposite of this is also true.

4. It takes years to build your reputation, seconds to destroy it.
Make integrity your number one priority & currency. People take chances on people they can depend on.

5. Paper everything. 
Buyers are liars. Everyone lies. Everything is great until money is involved. Even if it's your life partner or your best friend, document everything as if they're a stranger. Anyone who refuses to do this with you is a red flag. Run away.

6. Listen more, talk less. 
Nobody knows everything, we're all figuring it out. If you're just starting, you literally know nothing. Everything is a learning opportunity.

7. Being overpowering is not the same as having power. 
Don't be an a$$. Be helpful without being asked & expect nothing in return. Do a great job. Take the high road. Earn respect. People don't have to like you, but they will respect you.

8. You don’t matter to anyone important until you do.
Nobody will help you be successful until you can at least do the same for them. Not your friends, partners, investors. It’ll just be you for a while.

9. Learn to apologize even when it's not your fault. 
Sometimes it's more important to make the other person feel good than to be right.

10. Your gut is probably right. 
You can still try it, just know when to walk away. Every minute spent on the wrong thing is a minute you're not spending on the right thing.

11. Know what your ultimate goal is. 
Say yes to things that take you closer to it and no to things that don't. No exceptions.

12. There's no playbook. 
Some people work hard and get nothing. Some people do very little and get everything. Everyone has circumstances you know nothing about. Comparison is the thief of joy. Honor your path.

13 It's fu*king hard. 
Everything will cost more in time, money, and relationships than you think. It's just how it is. There's nothing wrong with you. Businesses are built in decades, not years.

14. It'll all be worth it when you get there. 
Don't forget to celebrate the small wins along the way, and stop to smell the flowers. You can't control the destination, so the journey is all there is.
