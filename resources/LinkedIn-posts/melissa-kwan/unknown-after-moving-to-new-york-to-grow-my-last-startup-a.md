After moving to New York to grow my last startup and to build a new life, I kept my apartment in Vancouver because I was scared to let go of my old life.

I was confiding in a friend about how hard it was to make new friends and settle into my new city. 

💡 He told me, "You suffer because you're trying to live in the past and the future. Either choose the past and don't look forward. Or choose the future and never look back. But you cannot have both."

I left that conversation, called my landlord, gave up that apartment, sold everything and never looked back.

Moving to New York changed my life's trajectory, and was the best decision I've ever made. 

It triggered butterfly effect that led to the sale of my last company, meeting my life partner (David) who became my cofounder years later, and the reason we nomaded for 3 years and found home again in Amsterdam.
