I cofounded eWebinar intentionally as a "lifestyle business". What does that mean? It means a lot more than not raising VC.

To us, it means:
1. Choosing happiness over revenue for all decisions, such as:
• Pricing - Low price point, no enterprise plans
• Support - Email and chat only, no phone
• Product - Something that excites me
• GTM - Product-led, no salespeople
• Operations - Remote team

2. Ensuring that everyone involved, not just the founders, has a great lifestyle for contributing their time & effort:
• No holiday policy
• No set work hours
• Higher than market rate salaries
• Encourage days off and "me" days
• No meetings, no micromanagement

The industry has given “lifestyle business” such a bad reputation by commonly using it to describe non-venture scalable businesses with "less ambitious" founders. (BTW, starting a business is in itself, ambitious. Don't let anyone tell you different.)

"This is fine as a lifestyle business.” Says every VC with a slight look of disdain after you pitch them your idea they don’t want to invest in. I heard of my fair share of this when I was trying to raise for my last startup -- my journey I talked about in detail on S1E1 of my podcast, ProfitLed, and the experience that made me an avid bootstrapper.

Only 1% of startups are venture funded, which means the other 99% are bootstrapped; and by industry standard, "lifestyle businesses". 

🦄 If anything between zero and unicorn is “lifestyle”, I’d happily take it.

My cofounder (and life partner) and I get up everyday without an alarm clock, work whenever we want/don’t want, and go to all the fun things around the world that our friends invite us to. We get to enjoy the fruits of our labor while building our startup; I wouldn’t trade this lifestyle for anything, especially not a capital injection and the strings that come with it.
