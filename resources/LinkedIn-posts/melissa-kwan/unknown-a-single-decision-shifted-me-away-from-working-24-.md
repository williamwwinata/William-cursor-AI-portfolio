A single decision shifted me away from working 24/7 to designing my life around around maximizing happiness and enjoyment. 🤯 

In 2017, David and I left New York to travel full time as “digital nomads”. I was running my last startup, Spacio, and he was a fractional CTO. We both worked remotely so we thought that instead of paying $3500 for a room in the Manhattan, we’d redirect those funds to renting Airbnbs around the world.

That was the first decision I made FOR ME since I became an entrepreneur in 2011.

After years of struggling, chasing debt, and paying other people’s bills, Spacio finally broke even and I started paying myself a meager salary. At last, I was coming out of survival mode and had the mindshare to ask myself, "If building this company would continue to be hard, what is something I could do for me?"

I hadn’t traveled much up to that point and always wanted to. I pitched the idea to David and he hopped on board immediately. We put a few boxes in storage and took off.

That period of nomading lasted 3 years and it was the most profound experience of our lives – it was a journey of exploration and self-development. We found Amsterdam, the place we now call home. We discovered music, partying, and community, the things that give us the most joy and contentment.

I finally realized that there was more to life than building companies. I had given up living for so long that I didn’t even remember what it was like to have fun.

I learned that working more doesn't mean you'll be more successful. I didn't need to work more, I needed to work more creatively. You can only do your best every day, and that is enough.

I started to work more normal hours and took evenings and weekends off. Guess what? Revenue did not take a hit, but my wellbeing significantly increased. 😎 

All my life up to that point, I thought my dream lifestyle was the end goal; the final reward for success. I was waiting to achieve it like unlocking another level of a video game. Had I continued to wait, it would have never happened.

Deciding to change my circumstance taught me the most important philosophy that has since formed the foundation of my life:

💡 THE ONLY WAY TO LIVE THE LIFE YOU WANT IS TO DESIGN IT.
