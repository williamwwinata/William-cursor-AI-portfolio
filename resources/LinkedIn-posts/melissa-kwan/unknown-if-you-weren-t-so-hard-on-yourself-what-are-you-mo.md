If you weren't so hard on yourself, what are you most proud of? 🙌 

Recently, there was a moment when I realized I only looked forward to see how much farther there was to go, and never backwards to see how far I’d come.

Once upon a time, I moved to New York to surround myself with more people like me who were doing cool things. It’s where the smartest, most successful people congregate because it’s New York! The greatest city in the world, and where the money is. 

For the first time, I met founders I deeply respected and aspired to become. Peers and friends of mine were featured on Techcrunch, raising tens (sometimes hundreds) of millions of dollars on a whim.

It was incredible to be in such close proximity to the success I wanted to have, but also soul crushing as I was trying so hard at the time and could barely get my startup off the ground. That said, I loved it because it fueled me.

However, that was a double-edged sword. When you’re around people who are achieving things that you are not, it’s impossible not to compare yourself to them. Over the years, I watched friends who were younger than me, spend less years on their businesses, and exit for 8 to 9 figures. These things often led me to question my own ability…

Am I not as smart as them? If yes, then why am I not as successful?

What if I never see the success that I want? Will it ever be my turn?

Should I keep going or am I wasting my time?

Am I even meant for this? What if I’m not? 😟

I’m aware that comparison is the thief of joy. But we’re entrepreneurs and we wouldn’t be doing this if we didn’t have big egos. My ego wants me to win too.

I’ve been blessed to be surrounded by a community of founder friends that I look up to and learn from, who have been pillars in my journey. While I’m happy for their success, it makes me feel like a complete failure.

I’ve had some successes in the last decade, but not enough to feel at peace, so every day feels like a rat race. There’s always another level to unlock, another growth hack, another million ARR. I am always chasing the next thing, unable to be happy and content with where I am today.

💡Last month, I was catching up with an old friend who gave me the reality check I needed:

I am living the life of freedom today that I set out to find 15 years ago when I walked out of my cubicle at SAP. 

That is something I am incredibly proud of.

It’s hard to see progress when you’re in it. Just because you haven’t seen success the way you’ve wanted, doesn’t mean you’re not successful at all.
