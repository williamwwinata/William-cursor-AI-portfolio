We spent 6 months developing the most complicated and toughest add-on feature we've ever built that none of our competitors even come close to having. This email came in right after we released it. 🫣 

Consumer expectations are at an all time high, and if you don't have everything, it's getting tougher to get people to sign up for anything. I don't envy anyone starting a software company right now. 

When customers want a discount or expect something for free, the knee jerk reaction is to give it to them to keep them happy. But, is that the best thing to do for your business? 🧐 

This feature we built (universal dashboard across all webinars) was designed for mid to large sized companies that are power users of our product. The company who sent this message is not one of those companies. Our product works perfectly fine without this add-on, they just want it...but not enough to pay for it. When people complain, it's important to assess who/where that information is coming from.

We didn't start eWebinar to be the cheapest, we started it to be the best. Prospects don't convert and customers leave us every day because we're "too expensive" and that's okay!

It's more important to find and serve customers who value us enough to invest in our solution over another. If someone can't justify the ROI, we let them go.

You cannot price according to someone's ability to pay.

Your value doesn't decrease based on someone's inability to see your worth.
