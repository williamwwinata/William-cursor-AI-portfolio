I made every mistake imaginable in my last startup. It took 2.5 years of pivoting before we made our first $10. 🥵 To say it was an uphill battle is an understatement.

Our product, Spacio, was an open house check-in system that replaces pen and paper sign-in for real estate agents. It could not be more niche, and revenue potential could not be more limited.

Of the 1.5m agents in the US, only a small subset of those would host open houses as part of their business, and a tinier subset of those would be tech savvy and willing enough to switch to digital. 

The real estate industry is regionalized, which meant our product was only applicable to the US market. When we were struggling to find product-market fit, there was no way to test other industries without significant changes to the product which we didn't have the runway to do.

The hardest thing about that startup was that I had no experience, yet falsely believed I did because I had a previous business that did alright. A lot of bad decisions were made because I was too confident, underestimating how difficult it would be to find a product someone would pay for month over month.

We brute forced our way to revenue, broken even a year after we made our first dollar, and got acquired another year later. 💰

By living through what not to do, I learned how to choose businesses with great potential, how to compete for revenue as the new kid on the block, and why the ONLY people who need to give you money are your customers. Building that business was the best education I could’ve never paid for.

💡 There were 10 pivotal Aha! Moments that got us to profitability and acquisition while owning 94% of our company – an exit that gave me the confidence and capital to found eWebinar 2 months post sale on my own terms.
