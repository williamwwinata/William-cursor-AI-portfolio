What's a power you never embraced because you, or someone else, limited your ability? 🧐

I was born left-handed, but my parents made me switch because they didn’t want me to smudge my homework (back when we wrote with this thing called a pencil). ✏️

I was so young I didn't remember it. But they once told the story of how I was ambidextrous and did schoolwork with both hands because they found it cute.

Growing up, I was often confused about which hand to use. Both felt natural for some things but left for some and right for others. I never questioned it as that was my normal.

Recently, I started wondering if switching from left to right could’ve disrupted my natural development. A quick Google search told me that it could cause behavioral, cognitive, speech issues from brain confusion.

I’ve always felt more creative than I was “supposed” to be (for a right-hander). I loved writing as a kid and aced art throughout high school. I invented games and assigned friends roles they didn’t ask for 😂…and eventually even started companies! 

When I told a friend this story, she suggested that I retrain my right brain and reclaim the artist I never got to fully become.

💡 That's when it hit me:

I thought I was "more creative", but I would've never considered myself an artist. I thought an artist was someone who painted or made things you could touch and feel.

At that moment, I wondered: What if I AM an artist, and words are my medium? How would I approach writing differently?

I've always been good with words and communicating ideas, but I told myself I wasn't because "English is my second language."

I started to write on LinkedIn 3 years ago as a way to market my business, but it's one of the few things I enjoy doing most. Looking back at all the letters, stories, and heartfelt/inspirational messages I’ve written over the years, I realized…

I am an artist who never embraced her power and cultivated her craft. Now, instead of treating my writing as a business need, I write as a form of expression. Instead of doing it from a place of necessity, I do it from a place of love because it feels good.

We all carry limiting beliefs, some that were never ours to begin with:

"Right-handers aren't creative."

"Your English sucks because it's not your first language."

"You're not an artist if you don't paint."

In order to fully grow into our own, we must unlearn what goes against who we're meant to be. This allows us to live in alignment with what feels true.
