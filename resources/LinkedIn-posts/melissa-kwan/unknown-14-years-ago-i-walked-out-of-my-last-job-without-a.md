14 years ago, I walked out of my last job without a plan other than wanting to forge my own destiny. 💪

I was the boldest sales exec on my team and made 10x the cold calls anyone else. I was hired without any previous experience so I knew I had to work harder than my peers.

I was given the most difficult set of accounts because my boss knew that if anyone else had it, they would quit. I was up for the challenge because I loved being surrounded by really smart people which I was never exposed to before that job. 

The only thing that kept me there were learning opportunities because I wasn’t making commission on top of my base salary, while others on my team were hitting their on-target earnings with less effort. 😐

One day, I was told I couldn't attend a workshop I had been looking forward to for weeks because the Head of HR was visiting from HQ, and my boss needed his best dialer to be there for his dog and pony show. 

The next day, I quit because he robbed me of my opportunity to learn and the reason I showed up. I wrote “freedom is being true to who you really are” on my whiteboard, and walked out. 🚶🏻‍♀️

I vowed that I would never be someone’s pawn again. I had no idea how to start a business, I just knew that freedom was my number one priority and I would do whatever it took to figure it out.

Recently, someone asked me to share a moment in my journey that I'm most proud of...

💡 That moment is RIGHT NOW.

I left my cubicle that day and decided to design the life that I want to live. I set out to find a career with limitless potential that would allow me to work from anywhere in the world. I had no idea what that life would look like, I just wanted to go there.

After all these years, I can finally say that I am now living the life I once dreamt about.

I have 100% control over my schedule, a growing business that pays me a good salary (for the first time ever this year), work with a remote team that self-manages, a thriving social life, and a loving partner who’s also my cofounder. While I don’t have everything I want, my life is not lacking.

It took a long time to get here. There were so many ups and downs. This has been nothing short of a great adventure.

I've arrived. And yet, rather than feeling like I've reached my destination, it feels like the start of something more spectacular. 💫
