You don't need to offer a discount. You don't need to extend your trial period. You don't need to give a paid product away for free. 🙅‍♀️ 

Every month, we have customers who churn because they found a cheaper product. Users who don't convert because they think we're too expensive. Prospects who ask for discounts before signing up.

We didn't start this company to be the cheapest. We started this company to be the best.

15 years ago, Dropbox spread like wildfire with their freemium product and that gave us the impression that free = more users. It deluded startups to think that giving something away for free is a go-to-market strategy, when it’s far from that.

Giving away something for free has many negative implications that aren’t often talked about. The most irritating one being the fact that it attracts the worst customer profiles. The people who are least tech savvy, most time consuming on support, and never end up converting due to price sensitivity.

😤 There were a few types of users who never activated on our platform no mattter how hard we tried:
• People who signed up because of a discount or extended trial
• Partners/affiliates, influencers, and friends who got free accounts
• Accounts we do all the setting up for (for free) in hopes to get them going

It's a knee jerk reaction to price match just to keep a customer. It's tempting to give someone with a big audience a free account especially when they promise you the world. But it doesn't help you if they never use it.

When people don't value your service enough to pay for it or put effort into setting it up, it means they also don't value your service enough to learn it and integrate it into their business. We found that it hurt us more because we took away their urgency from getting started.

We no longer give discounts and we don't give away free accounts. Instead, we focus on serving customers who know we are motivated to invest time and resource in our solution. If someone can't justify the ROI, we let them go.

You cannot price according to someone's ability to pay.

Your value doesn't decrease based on someone's inability to see your worth.
