Companies like Amazon and Costco who pride themselves on customer service conditioned consumers to think that they're are always right.

So, are they?! I don't think so. If the customer's always right, you'll be in a world of pain. 😩

As a bootstrapped company, we put *so much* into building an incredible product experience and responding to every question as quickly as we can because that's what is required to keep our customers and win new ones.

But, in the faceless world of software, customer demands can be brutal, leading to soul crushing and demoralizing support. We've fired multiple customers over being rude to our team. Yes, we're the vendor, but a heathy business relationship has to be built on mutual respect such that everyone can feel appreciated and valued.

🎙️ On ProfitLed Podcast S2E18, "The Customer is NOT Always Right", my COO and I recount our customers from hell, the things they did to try and get around paying for our service, and how we dealt with demanding customers.

👉 Key takeaways:
• What happens to your business when the customer is always right
• Customer horror stories and what they did to game the system
• How we set expectations for customer relationships
• How to deal with customers from hell
