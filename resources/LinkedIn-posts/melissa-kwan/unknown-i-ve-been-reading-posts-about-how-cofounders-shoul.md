I've been reading posts about how cofounders shouldn't split equity 50/50 because one person has to be “in control”. I strongly disagree. ✋

Seeing the number of people who got behind that sentiment (perhaps blindly) inspired me to share my story in my newsletter a couple days ago.

I don’t agree that the intention behind cofounder equity split should be about “power” – if that is your starting point, maybe you have the wrong cofounder. Or, maybe you are the wrong cofounder.

Building a company together is not about exercising control over each other’s decisions. Critical decisions are made over intelligent, informed, and respectful discussions based on each person’s expertise, and complete trust from everyone else that the person making that decision is, in fact, the best person to make it.

Rather than being about “control”, equity split should be about what is fair. Just because you are cofounders, does not mean your responsibilities are weighted 50/50. A business and technical cofounder have vastly different capabilities, and vastly different responsibilities as a result.

I learned this the hard way previous to eWebinar, where I built the majority of the business but my cofounder made out financially better than me post acquisition. The gradual realization of our tremendously unequal sacrifice and impact brewed enough resentment to eventually break a would’ve-been beautiful friendship between two people who spent an era together. (Before you start throwing stones, please take a moment to read my story.)

Nothing matters until money is on the table, and when the deal is in front of you, you bet you’d wish things were equitable for the years you put into your company.

There are many different ways to split a pie. I’m not saying that I have the magic formula. But as someone who lived through this, I can tell you how much it sucks to contribute significantly more and get equal (or less) of the deal.

On my newsletter, I shared my personal story in detail and what I did differently with my cofounder at eWebinar to avoid the same situation.
