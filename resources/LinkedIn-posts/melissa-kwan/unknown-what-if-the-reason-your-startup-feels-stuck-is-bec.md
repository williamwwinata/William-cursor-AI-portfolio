What if the reason your startup feels stuck is because *you* feel stuck? 🫣 

A year ago, I found myself unable to get my sh*t together and show up to work with the same inspiration that I used to have. I gave myself permission to slow down so I could return to the business in full force when I felt ready again…but that day never came.

Since work has always been a core part of my identity and where I derived a sense of strength, my lack of confidence was bleeding into every other aspect of my life: my relationship, my friendships, my everyday life. 

Last month, through a friend's recommendation, I spent a week at the Hoffman Process (self-development retreat) to shake things up. 

It was most profound and impactful self-growth experience I’ve ever had. It was an intense week of learning things about myself I wasn’t attuned to, gaining clarity into the driving forces behind my behaviors, and breakthroughs as to how new decisions can be made moving forward if I want to feel joy, gratefulness, and success.

💡 I shared this story in my latest newsletter, including:
• How understanding yourself relates to building companies
• The negative self-talk in my head that lead to my ultimate procrastination
• My most shocking limiting belief that I would've never come to realize on my own

If you asked me a month ago, "Do you love yourself?" I would've said, without a doubt, "Of course!" I don't hate myself, so I must love myself, right?

But, someone who love themselves would not tell themselves that they're not smart enough, not good enough, not doing enough. Founders are notorious for being harsh on themselves. How can we not be when we're surrounded by other founders who seemingly know what they’re doing, more than ourselves?

This is not a story that comes with a silver bullet. This is a story about looking inwards if you feel a bit stuck right now.

🙌  Through awareness, comes choice. Through choice, comes change.
