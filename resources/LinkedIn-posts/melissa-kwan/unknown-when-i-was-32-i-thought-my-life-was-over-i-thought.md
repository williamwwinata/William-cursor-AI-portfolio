When I was 32, I thought my life was "over". I thought I needed to settle down, get married, have kids, buy a house, and stop doing fun stuff. 😥 

I was living in Vancouver, surrounded by friends I grew up with...friends who chose a different path. They were in 9-5 jobs and starting families while I was trying to get my startup off the ground and struggling to pay rent.

I found myself drifting farther and farther away from them. My safe haven no longer felt like home.

That's when I started traveling to NYC every other month in hopes to find like-minded people. I wanted to move there full time but the idea of starting over in my mid-30s with no friends was daunting. I didn't want to leave behind the life I built.

After contemplating for 1.5 years, I finally took that leap.

Little did I know that one week later, I would trigger the butterfly effect 🦋 that changed everything. I became the cofounder of a co-living space in Manhattan, met the love of my life (our first Airbnb guest) who would become my cofounder to eWebinar years later, ended up nomading the world for 3 years, then found community and home again in Amsterdam.

During my time NYC, my last startup took off which led to its acquisition in 2019. I had some of the best years of my life even at the lowest point of my career.

We have this unexplainable, unjustified, and unsettling fear around change and disrupting the security of our status quo.

💡 But everything great is truly on the other side of fear.

I am just one example of someone who only changed where she woke up in the morning, and ended up changing her entire life's trajectory.

This was the farewell FB post I wrote at the airport: 

"If even a small part of you wonder whether you too, can embark on a journey into the unknown, I would like to leave you with this: 

There exists within all of us a dreamer and an explorer. When we allow them to find each other, we are at our most powerful. Let your curiosity and quest for knowledge pave the way, and may that become the story we will one day share."

To all the dreamers out there reading this -- THIS IS YOUR TIME. 💫
