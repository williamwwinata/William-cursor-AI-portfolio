The 5 most important things we are learning on our way to $2m ARR: 😳

1. Increasing prices has been the biggest revenue driver. 💰
2 years ago, we doubled our prices but kept them the same for legacy customers. Some founders advised me to raise prices for everyone as that would be the fastest way to profitability, especially if the new pricing reflected the product’s value. I didn't listen because I wanted to do the honorable thing. A year later, under pressure to invest in growing platform costs and hiring, we bit the bullet and increased prices for legacy customers. The result was a 29% boost in revenue without noticeable churn.

2. Meaningful month-over-month growth got much harder. 📈
Our climb to $1m ARR was a consistent and predictable 36 months. In the first 1.5 years, we'd see 10-20% MoM revenue growth. As time went on, we'd see 5-10%. After $1m, we never saw double-digit MoM growth outside of our price increase. Customers spend more with us especially since we now offer add-ons, but that also means we lose more revenue if they churn. 

3. Product and business outgrew marketing. 😐
As we released features, collected customer stories, and discovered new use cases, we added them to the first version of our website if we could find a place for them. Over time, our marketing site became harder and harder to understand because it was pieced together – and didn’t even highlight our most compelling features! Our marketing didn’t reflect the product we had grown into. We spent 9 months redoing our messaging after in-depth customer interviews, and gave our website a facelift to better communicate the value and functionalities we could deliver.

4. The learning never stops. 🧑‍🏫
As customers integrate our product into their workflows and find new use cases for it, we are constantly learning how to make our product better and stickier for them. As we move through different phases of our business, and as the market, economy, and consumer behaviors evolve, we need to find new ways to generate demand and stay top of mind. While learning keeps things from getting boring, it can also be exhausting.

5. $1m ARR was NOT the magic number. 🪄
Everyone refers to $1m ARR as the indication that you've found product market fit and are "ready to scale" by pouring fuel on the fire. We’re way past that, and still don’t feel like we’ve fully figured out product messaging or marketing fit. ”Ready to scale" seems like a pipe dream. It also turns out that $83k/month isn’t a lot of money. There's always another expense, another hire, another investment – and we're capital efficient with outsourcing and contractors! There’s not much extra “fuel” to pour on the fire even if we wanted to.
