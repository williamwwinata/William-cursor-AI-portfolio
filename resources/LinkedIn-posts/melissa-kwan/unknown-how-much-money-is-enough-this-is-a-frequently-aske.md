How much money is enough? This is a frequently asked question amongst my community of founders at all stages.🤑 

I grew up in a materialistic culture where you're judged by what you have. My dad took me window shopping to educate me on the most expensive brands. I remember flipping through the latest issue of Robb Report with him, looking at luxury homes, mega yachts, and super cars; all the things I could buy if I had the money.

My dad worked in the same company for 40 years, and my mom was a housewife. We were the epitome of middle class. They didn't come from money so they overcompensated by hoarding stuff they could show off to other people. This way of life really messed up my idea of success. I desperately wanted to be rich so I could have all the things.

My parents didn't want me to suffer like they did, so they gave me everything they had and more. I had the coolest gadgets and the biggest allowance. I thought money came easy because for me, it did. I didn't respect it.

It wasn't until I started my own company that I learned how difficult it'd be to make millions. I read about $100m exits and thought it was a rite of passage for startups. Reality slapped me in the face and I realized that I wasn’t going to have my turn. I wasn’t cut out for it. I discovered a newfound respect for money, mostly the amount of effort and dedication it takes to make it.

The irony is, I started a company to be rich. After a decade of bootstrapping, I learned to live with very little. What gave me the most joy was experience, adventure, and meaningful connections - not stuff.

I sold my last startup for a modest amount, and yet it changed my life drastically. The question changed from, "How much do I want to end up with?" to "How much is enough?"

A lot of my peers came to understand too that you don't need a lot to live large. We're all asking ourselves the same question because we don't want to spend years and years slogging away for our companies.

The problem with money is nobody talks about it. The result is you have no idea what to do with it and how to approach it.

In the last year, I've actively asked my friends how money has changed their lives, what they do with it, and what's enough.
