Self-service SaaS is the hardest thing I've ever built. I'd say 10x harder than sales-led.

Before I started my first company, I was already doing cold outreach for other people selling everything under the sun. Learning early that the only way to make a sale was to get in front of someone and convince them they need what you’re selling.

When I started my own companies, I kept doing the same thing, for myself. I had 10 years of hunting down decision makers, navigating months-long complex sales cycles, and doing whatever it took to get a deal across the line. Eventually, I was living at conferences, manning booths, and trading business cards.

I did what I had to do. But I didn't loved it. Every event was another hit to the one thing I care about most: The freedom to design my own life.

For years, I dreamt of a different kind of company. One where people could find you on their own, try the product, and become paying customers without ever getting on a call with you. I wanted to build a Calendly, Mailchimp, Basecamp. A business that didn’t require my physical presence to grow, and didn’t hold my calendar hostage to my customers’ demo schedule.

That dream was how I arrived at eWebinar. It checked every one of my "10 non-negotiables" box. The first thing was, “Must be able to be sold 100% through the internet. No more conferences."

Six years after the founding that company, I am humbled in a way I never anticipated. Building a self-service SaaS company is harder than anything I did in over a decade I spent selling enterprise software. I’d go as far as to say product-led is 10x harder than sales-led.

💡 The other side of that: The lifestyle is 10x more rewarding when it works.

When it works, you wake up when you want and work on what matters. No demos, no check-in calls, noone dictating your schedule. Revenue builds while you sleep. Nothing beats having zero calls on your calendar.

It’s the toughest nut I haven’t fully cracked. But I know that self-service SaaS is the pinnacle of my happiness.

What makes it so hard? I wish someone had told me before I started. It wouldn’t have changed my course, but it would’ve prepared me better.

1. A great cofounder is not optional 🤝
2. The math works against you from day one 🧮
3. Hiring budget won't match your standards 💸
4. Marketing becomes your entire life 📢
5. Customer success is the thing that keeps people at your party 🎉
6. Slow growth is a test of everything you’ve got 😰
7. AI is changing the rules of an already hard game 🤖

The challenge isn't a single one on that list. It’s all of them happening at the same time.

It’s doing everything the business needs simultaneously, well enough that growth consistently outpaces churn, month after month, for years.
