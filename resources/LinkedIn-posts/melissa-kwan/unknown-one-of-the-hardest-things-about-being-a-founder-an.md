One of the hardest things about being a founder and putting yourself out there is knowing what advice to follow, and what to ignore. 

Everyone has an opinion about how you should run your business based on their own experience. You can ask 10 people the same thing and get 10 different perspectives.

When I started writing on here daily 2 years ago, someone said to me, "Every day is too much, people will find you annoying." 😶‍🌫️ 

When I took a break from writing, I got DMs saying, "Where did you go?" 🕵 

When I wrote longer posts/articles less frequently..."You should write shorter posts more often." 😵‍💫 

So, what do I do? I'm open to ideas but take everything with a grain of salt.

I'm the only person who has to live with the consequences of my decisions. I only do things I want to do, never because someone else thinks it's a great idea. I do what I believe is right with conviction and no regrets.

When it comes to writing, I don't write (purely) for eyeballs. I write about what inspires me and comes to me naturally. There's a certain style that works for me, and the audience who cares about and connects with my content will spend time reading it. I don't write strategically for marketing and engagement - that has only ever had the opposite effect because it never comes off authentic.

This is just one example, as others are always enthusiastic about giving advice on all things from sales to marketing to operations. It comes from a place of good intentions, but can induce confusion and uncertainty as we also doubt ourselves!

In life and in business, there are many different ways to get to the same place. There are very few things you can't recover from. There's no right or wrong way, but there is a right way FOR YOU.

💡  Remember the story about the man, the boy, and the donkey? Please all, and you will please none.
