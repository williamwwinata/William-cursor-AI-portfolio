9 things I wish I knew as a founder 10 years ago 👩‍🏫: (What I learned from NOT going to Harvard.)

1. A great coder ≠ A great cofounder. 😴 
A great coder can get the product built, but it's not enough. A great cofounder is one who helps you sleep better at night.

2. If you want to make money, build a better lightbulb. 💡 
My first two startups were blue ocean opportunities, both were grueling uphill battles.

3. Any kind of exit is a great first exit. 💰 
A few hundred thousand dollars can change your life and bring you all the comforts you need so you can start something else. It’s much faster than trying to capture a unicorn.

4. You don’t have to stick with a career path just because you have experience in it. 🧑‍🏭 
Figure out what makes you happy and find a career that serve that. It’s a lot easier to learn the skills to make it success than to find happiness later when you’re stuck in something.

5. Businesses shouldn’t be that hard. 😰 
I embraced the suck for so long because I glorified the place I was in, thinking I needed to suffer in order to succeed. Instead of re-evaluating my business’ viability every couple months, I tried harder to push a mediocre business forward.

6. You don’t need anyone else’s validation to feel successful. 🙌 
Whether someone believes in you or not has no influence on your success. Your ability to win is not determined by someone else's inability to see it.

7. The only version of success that matters is your own. 😎 
 I cared too much about other people’s opinion of me and that was the cause of all my pain and discontent. I never felt like I was enough.

8. Get fluent in financial literacy. 👩‍🏫 
Your decade-long hard earned exit could be just around the corner, and if you’re not intentional about wealth preservation, you could lose a lot of it in a matter of months.

9. Your gut is always right. ✅ 
If something feels off from the start, it probably is. You don’t always need proof to walk away from something.
