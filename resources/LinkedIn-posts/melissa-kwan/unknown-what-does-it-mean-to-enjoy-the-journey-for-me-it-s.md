What does it mean to "enjoy the journey"? For me, it's not about the process of learning to building companies. 👣

I quit my last job 15 years ago to start my first business so I could be rich. Little did I know that starting a company was going to be the quickest way to spend all my money. 💸

This road has been difficult and frustrating. Many people have told me that I should just "enjoy the journey." I used to think that was something people said to console me for not being where I want to be.

Only in the last couple years did I truly come to understand the meaning of that statement.

I moved to New York to grow my last startup. The same week I moved there, I met David, my life partner. We had a blast living there for 3 years and still talk about it to this day.

Once that company hit profitability, we left New York to nomad for 3 years. During that time, we discovered music, community, and evolved together into who we are today. Since then, we've lived Amsterdam, Hong Kong, and Bangkok.

Because we're fully remote, we've been able to live a life of freedom, have full control over our schedules so we could say yes to all the fun stuff and deepen friendships by showing up.

My last company brought me to David, and I cofounded eWebinar with him 5 years ago. We have full vision and mission alignment. We have fun and work together 24/7. It's the greatest feeling in the world.

These were the things that brought me immense joy and none of them had anything to do with the day to day operations of startups. It had nothing to do with finding product market fit, increasing activation and lowering churn. It had nothing to do with how much revenue we've generated and how much I learned about SaaS businesses. 

I used to think "enjoy the journey" was about being content at every moment in time despite being "unsuccessful". That was never something I've ever been able to do. I am grateful but discontent, and that's the fuel for my fire.

Now, I know that the "journey" actually refers to the adventure of a lifetime my startups have taken me on. I loved every stop along the way to my eventual destination, wherever that is. I live a richer life today than most people who are financially better off than me.

🪄 The journey is not just your career. It's also the life your career enables.

ENJOY THE JOURNEY.
