Most companies think the answer to more revenue is to sell enterprise, but they aren't aware of the massive hassle and costs that come with going upmarket. 💸 

After 13 years of selling large enterprises and building two startups around it, I swore I would never do it again. I couldn't wait to start a new company that offers a low cost, self-serve SaaS product.

There's no such thing as free money. The bigger the deal, the more attention you need to give your customer. The more customizations you offer, the more versions of your product you have to maintain.

💰 Money and effort are directly related. More money means:
1. More time spent per deal: 1on1 outreach, long complex sales cycles
2. In-person sales: conferences, trade shows, co-selling with other vendors
3. High-touch customer support: dedicated AE, phone support, product customizations

More effort means more time and more people required to sell and build a product that enterprises will even consider buying.

I never wanted to sell enterprise again because it infringed on the lifestyle that I wanted. I didn't want to take anymore calls, travel for work, and do conferences. There's nothing worse than spending an exorbitant amount of money to attend a trade show and sit at a booth pretending to be enthusiastic.

Many founders think the air is better up there because they think it'll solve their money problems. 

But if you aren't ready to commit the energy and resources it takes to close, nurture and retain high value customers who are paying you to take care of them, you might be in for a surprise.
