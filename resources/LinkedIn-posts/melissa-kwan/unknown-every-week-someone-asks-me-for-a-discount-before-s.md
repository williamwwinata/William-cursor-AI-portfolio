Every week, someone asks me for a discount before signing up. Startups who say they're cost conscious. Non-profits want a break. A prospect who wants us to price match a competitor. 💸 

I say no every single time. But that wasn't always the case...

When we first launched, we had a 30-day trial and a $49/month plan. We thought lower prices and longer trials would bring in more customers. Instead, we got people who signed up and forgot about us. Who churned in a month and asked for refunds. They were the loudest in support and the least likely to convert.

When we shortened our trial, conversion went up 20%. When we doubled our prices, revenue went up 30%. The most demanding customers disappeared.

😶‍🌫️ Over time, we noticed that the users who never activated on our platform fell into these categories:
• People who signed up because of a discount or extended trial
• Partners, affiliates, and influencers who got free accounts in exchange for marketing
• Well-known companies we did all the setup for in hopes of accelerating their onboarding

Here's what I learned: when people don’t pay for something (whether it's using money or time), they don’t value it enough to invest into learning it. When there’s no investment, there’s no urgency. When there’s no urgency, there’s no action.

We focus on serving customers who are motivated to invest in our solution because they believe in it. If someone can’t justify the ROI, we let them go.

People will always ask for discounts. If you don’t ask, you don’t get. That’s just human nature. Someone asking doesn’t mean you need to give one.

Most of the time, they’re not saying you’re not worth it. They’re saying they don’t want to pay what you’re charging. They ask because you’re a startup and they think you’ll budge because you’re small. Nobody goes to Stripe and asks for a discount before signing up. Stripe wouldn’t even respond.

💡You cannot price according to someone’s ability to pay. Your value doesn’t decrease based on someone’s inability to see your worth. If you don’t value yourself, no one else will.
