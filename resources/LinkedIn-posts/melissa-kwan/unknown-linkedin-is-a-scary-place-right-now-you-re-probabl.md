LinkedIn is a scary place right now. You're probably thinking: Holy sh*t, everyone is crushing it in AI except me! 😵‍💫

Or...is it just me thinking that? 😂 

In all seriousness, AI companies are popping up everywhere. Everyone seems to be an expert. The technology landscape has already changed and we have no idea where it's headed and how fast. GTM motions that used to work well, don't anymore. 

We're now living in a time where a startup can hit $100m ARR in 12 months. W T F?! 🤯  Tech companies are scared right now. I am too.

Every day, I ask myself if we're doing something wrong. Did I choose the wrong problem to solve 5 years ago? Why didn't I see this coming? Is there some way we can make this wave work for us or would that be too distracting? 

How quickly could someone else build what we have right now and how would that impact our chances of getting acquired? What's going to be our competitive advantage in the age of everything-automation?!

There are a lot of things I don't know right now, but what I DO know is *a bird in hand is worth two in the bush*. 🐦

I know that we spent 5 years building a business that is finally profitable and everyone is happy.

I know that business is is only getting harder as consumers expectations are rising exponentially.

I know that if we chase after shiny objects, we'd risk f*cking up what we've worked so hard to build.

It's so easy to get distracted right now because it feels like everyone is hopping on that train but you. There are 70k AI companies. There's 1 Cursor and 1 Lovable.

🙌  At the end of the day, the same core principles of a sustainable business still applies:
1. You have unique insight in the business you chose.
2. You're solving a real problem for people you believe in.
3. Your revenue covers your cost, you have time to figure it out.

Don't take your eye off the ball. Don't f*ck it up. It's not worth it.
