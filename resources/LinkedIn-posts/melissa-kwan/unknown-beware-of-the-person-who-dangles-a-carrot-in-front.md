Beware of the person who dangles a carrot in front of you, be it a potential investor or customer. 🥕 

In my last startup, it took 2.5 years of product iterations before the first person paid $10. We were always running out of money and had to take side projects to float the team. Back then, I’d talk to anyone who might help stretch our runway.

An industry friend had just sold his company and was making angel investments. He told me that if we got to $10k MRR, he'd invest $25k. At the time, that would've been a huge lifeline for us.

When we finally got there, I called him up, excited to claim the offer. He said, "I'm going to need to see more traction."

Angry and frustrated, I reminded him of his word. Rather than acknowledging he changed his mind (which would have been perfectly acceptable), he said, "Melissa, you shouldn't react this way, it makes you look bad."

I said, "No. This makes YOU look bad." We never spoke again.

In business, this happens a lot. An investor saying they'll put money in if you hit X. A prospect promising to buy if you build that one missing feature.

I can’t even count how many times someone has told me they’d subscribe to eWebinar if only we could match another product’s feature, then went silent when we finally built it.

💡 Truth is, you can't make decisions based someone who thinks you're only good enough IF...

Sometimes those things come through. Most times, they don't.

Focus on people willing to invest in you today. They’re the ones who already see your value.
