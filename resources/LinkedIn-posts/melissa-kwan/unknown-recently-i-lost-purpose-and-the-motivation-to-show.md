Recently, I lost purpose and the motivation to show up. 😰 I thought I hit "bored-out" and took 3 months off. But, that wasn't it.

After my last startup was acquired in 2019 for a non-retirement level exit, all I wanted next was to build a small company that could pay me $20k a month.

I was 37 and had never gotten a "market salary". I always made compromises and lived frugally. After a decade of chasing and being in survival mode, I just wanted to have a "normal life" like peers my age.

I wanted to go to restaurants and order without looking at the price first. I wanted the possibility of buying a home. I wanted to take holidays and attend destination weddings -- to make up for the ones I missed in my 20s.

I started eWebinar to be a micro-SaaS company. One that could be run with a small team, limited support, and one we could stop growing once it hits a certain number. The idea and product became much larger than anticipated and I gave up the idea of building a micro-company. It would've been irresponsible to stop pushing a business that's working.

4 years later (beginning of this year), we broke even. I started paying myself, then increased it every month as revenue grew. My number was hit, I was finally living comfortably. My drive dissipated. I stopped feeling the hustle and started feeling the exhaustion.

A couple weeks ago, an epiphany hit me. Steve Jobs said you can only connect the dots looking backwards. What if I could try and connect the dots forward? I'm a believer that you're exactly where you're supposed to be. This has been a special year. I'm at the peak of my professional and personal life. My social community exploded and it has become the most important thing in my life. It is the thing that gives me the most joy and excitement.

I have daydreams about a life that we can share with our friends every day. Whether that’s a new way of living at a compound or a building, I don’t know yet. What I do know is that to turn that dream into reality, we need significant capital.

I started eWebinar to give myself a good life, but I didn't need much to live large. The idea that we could channel eWebinar's success into a community project next time around reignited the fire I lost. I rediscovered purpose by imagining a life of impact that goes far beyond my own enjoyment.

💡 They say you can never do it just for the money -- turns out, they were right.

What is the thing that gets you out of bed and charges your soul?

What is going to be your legacy?
