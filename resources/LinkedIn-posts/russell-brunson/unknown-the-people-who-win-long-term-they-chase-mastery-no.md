"The people who win long term?
They chase mastery, not money.

Money is a byproduct.
Mastery is the engine.

Most people start a business asking:
“How do I make money?”

But the people who build something that lasts ask a different question:
“How do I get so good at solving this problem that people can’t ignore it?”

They study deeper.
Practice longer.
Refine their craft when no one’s watching.

Because when you pursue mastery:

• Your skills compound
• Your reputation grows
• Your opportunities multiply

And over time… the money follows.

Not because you chased it.
But because you became too valuable to overlook."
