Entrepreneurs don’t win because they have more resources.
They win because they’re more resourceful.

I still remember the first time I invested $1,000 in myself.

The problem?
I didn’t have $1,000 to spend.
Not comfortably. Not at all.

But I knew I had to get into that program.
So I got resourceful.
I grabbed a rake, knocked on doors, and started cleaning up yards, one leaf pile at a time.

Because I wasn’t just buying a course…
I was betting on myself.

That was the beginning of everything.

Since then, I’ve built companies, served millions of entrepreneurs, and helped others create their own first wins.

But it all started with that first leap, when logic said “no,” and resourcefulness said “go.”

You don’t need more capital.
You need more commitment.
