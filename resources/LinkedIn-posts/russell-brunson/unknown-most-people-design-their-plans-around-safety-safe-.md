Most people design their plans around safety.

Safe timelines.
Safe goals.
Safe decisions that won’t rock the boat.

But safety rarely creates breakthroughs.

The entrepreneurs who actually build something meaningful design their plans around possibility.

They ask different questions:

What could this become if it worked?
What would I attempt if failure wasn’t the biggest fear?
What kind of future is worth the risk of trying?

Possibility stretches you.
Safety contains you.

And the truth is, the biggest opportunities rarely look “safe” in the beginning.

They look uncertain.
Unproven.
A little intimidating.

But that’s usually the signal you’re heading somewhere worth going.

So when you’re designing your next move…
don’t just ask what feels safe.

Ask what’s possible.
