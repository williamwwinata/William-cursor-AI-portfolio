If your plan doesn’t require courage,
it won’t require growth either.

Safe plans protect your ego.
Bold plans stretch your identity.

Most people design goals they’re already qualified for.
Goals that don’t risk embarrassment.
Goals that don’t challenge their current skill set.
Goals that feel impressive… but comfortable.

But growth lives on the other side of discomfort.

Launching before you feel ready.
Raising your prices.
Hiring before it feels “safe.”
Speaking up when it would be easier to stay quiet.

If your plan doesn’t make you a little nervous…
It’s probably too small.

Courage is the toll you pay for expansion.
And the bigger the vision, the more it demands from you.

Is your current plan protecting who you are…
Or building who you’re becoming?
