Most professionals treat their inbox like it’s their job.

It’s not.

Your inbox is simply a collection of other people’s priorities, requests, and emergencies.

When you live there, you give up control of your most valuable asset: time.

A simple constraint changes everything.

When you limit how much time you spend on email, clarity shows up fast. The real priorities rise. The noise loses its grip.

Here’s the practical takeaway:
 •Your time needs intention, not availability
 •Unplanned time gets spent on low leverage work
 •Protecting focus is a leadership skill, not a productivity hack
