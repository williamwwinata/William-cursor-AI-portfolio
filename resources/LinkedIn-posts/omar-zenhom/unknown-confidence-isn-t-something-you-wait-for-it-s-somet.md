Confidence isn’t something you wait for. It’s something you practice.

One thing Cassandra said that stuck with me is how backwards most of us have it.

We think confidence comes first, then we change how we show up.

But it often works the other way around. You change how you show up… and confidence follows.

It’s not about a full makeover. It’s the small signals. Clothes that fit well. A look that feels intentional.

Those little shifts change how you carry yourself, how you speak, how you walk into a room.

Over time, you start acting like the person you’ve been trying to become.

Sometimes identity follows action.
