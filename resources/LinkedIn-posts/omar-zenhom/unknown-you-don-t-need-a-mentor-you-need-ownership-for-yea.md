You don’t need a mentor.
You need ownership.

For years, I thought the missing piece was the “right” person to guide me.

Someone smarter. More experienced. Someone who would just tell me what to do.

That person doesn’t exist.
Mentors can teach skills.
They can share frameworks.
They can offer perspective.

But they can’t choose your sacrifices.
They can’t define your goals.
And they definitely can’t remove uncertainty.
Clarity doesn’t come from waiting.

It comes from moving.
Ask for insight, not permission.
Build a council, not a savior.
And start trusting your own filter.

The strongest decisions I’ve ever made weren’t handed to me.

They were built.
