Trust is not built through charisma. It’s built through vulnerability.

In my conversation with Charles Duhigg, he explained that vulnerability is often misunderstood. It’s not dramatic oversharing. It's something smaller and more powerful.

It happens when you say something that could be judged.

Your brain immediately scans the other person for threat.

If they withhold judgment and respond with empathy, trust forms quickly.
If they judge, connection shuts down.

This is hardwired into us. It’s how we determine whether someone is safe.

In business, this matters more than we realize.

Negotiation, leadership, sales, even conflict resolution all hinge on whether the other person feels judged or understood.

We go deeper into how this works and how to apply it intentionally.
