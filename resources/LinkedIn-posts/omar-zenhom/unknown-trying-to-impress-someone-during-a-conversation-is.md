Trying to impress someone during a conversation is usually a mistake.

In my conversation with Charles Duhigg, he shared that strong communicators focus less on being clever and more on identifying what kind of discussion is happening.

There are three primary types:
• Practical conversations about solving problems
• Emotional conversations about feelings and empathy
• Social conversations about identity and relationships

All three are valid. The issue arises when two people are having different types of conversations at the same time. When that happens, connection breaks down.

Psychology calls this the matching principle. Successful communication requires alignment in the moment.
