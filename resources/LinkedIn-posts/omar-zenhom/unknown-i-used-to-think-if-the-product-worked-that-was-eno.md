I used to think if the product worked, that was enough. It’s not.

People don’t really care about what your product does on paper.

They care about what it feels like when they use it.

If it’s smooth, easy, reliable… they stay. If it’s frustrating, even a little, they’re gone.

We learned that the hard way. So we stopped adding more features and just focused on making the product feel better to use.

That one decision changed everything.

People stayed longer, revenue improved, and growth finally started to feel easier.

For entrepreneurs, this is the part that gets ignored way too often.
