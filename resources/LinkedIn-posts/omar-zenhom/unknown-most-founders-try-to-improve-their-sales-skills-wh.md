Most founders try to improve their sales skills when selling feels difficult. They rehearse scripts, tweak pricing, or work on confidence. That’s rarely the real problem.

When people hesitate to buy, it’s often a signal about perceived value. Not a personal flaw. Not a marketing failure. A clarity issue.

If the outcome is obvious and meaningful, selling becomes a conversation instead of a performance. The work shifts from convincing to communicating.

The episode walks through how to evaluate your offer honestly, spot where the value gap is, and rebuild from the inside out. It’s less about tactics and more about alignment.

If you want a cleaner, calmer approach to selling, check out the full episode on the $100 MBA Show. It breaks down the exact mindset shift that makes revenue feel less forced and more predictable.
