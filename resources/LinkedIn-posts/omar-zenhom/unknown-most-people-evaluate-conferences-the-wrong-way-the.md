Most people evaluate conferences the wrong way.

They look at the speaker lineup, the venue, or the Instagram clips. None of those determine whether the event will actually move your business forward.

What matters most is the room.
A great conference attracts people who are building, operating, and taking real action.

When you spend a few days surrounded by that energy, your standards rise automatically. Your thinking sharpens. Your ideas get clearer.

When the room is filled with spectators or people playing business, even world-class speakers can’t save the experience.

Here’s a simple way to evaluate any conference before you buy a ticket:
 •Look at who usually attends, not just who speaks
 •Ask whether the audience reflects where you want to be next
 •Pay attention to the types of conversations happening between sessions
