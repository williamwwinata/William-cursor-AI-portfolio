Most people think confidence is about what you say. It’s actually about pacing.

When we’re nervous, we speed up. We add more words, more explanation, more filler.

It feels productive in the moment, but it signals uncertainty. Slowing down has the opposite effect.

Pauses give your words weight and give the listener time to process. That creates authority without you trying to sound impressive.

If you want to apply this in conversations, presentations, or sales, start here:
Speak one sentence.
Pause.
Let it land.
Then continue.
