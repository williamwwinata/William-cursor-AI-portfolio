Most people don’t complain because they like negativity.

They complain because they don’t know what the next move is.
That’s the gap this episode addresses.

Complaints are often placeholders for unclear action. When that gap stays open, frustration repeats itself and momentum drops. Not dramatically. Gradually.

Key unlocks in this episode:
 •Complaints should trigger decisions, not conversations
 •Reframing a complaint into a question immediately changes behavior
 •Progress accelerates when emotion is acknowledged but not indulged

This is not about suppressing frustration. It’s about converting it into motion while it still has energy.
