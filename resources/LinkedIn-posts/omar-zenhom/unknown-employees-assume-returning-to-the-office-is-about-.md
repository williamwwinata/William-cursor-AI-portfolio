Employees assume returning to the office is about control.

In reality, it’s about momentum.

Businesses rarely fail because people aren’t working. They fail because decisions take too long, collaboration weakens, and energy drops across the team.

Remote work doesn’t create obvious problems. It introduces subtle delays that compound over time.

When teams are in the same space, decisions happen faster, ideas move quicker, and execution improves.

That’s why many large companies are shifting their approach.

They aren’t reacting emotionally. They’re removing friction that slows growth.
