Complaining isn’t logic.
It’s protection.

It’s your brain saying, “This isn’t my fault,” so you don’t have to change anything.

Talent, algorithm, economy, circumstances — pick your favourite excuse.

And yeah, it feels good for a minute.
That’s why people keep doing it.

But here’s the part nobody likes admitting:
it guarantees nothing changes.

It doesn’t solve the problem.
It doesn’t build confidence.
It doesn’t earn respect.
It trains your brain to look for what’s wrong instead of what’s possible.

This isn’t a pep talk.
It’s a cold splash of water.

And if you’re stuck in that loop — frustrated, angry, explaining — it’s also the way out.
