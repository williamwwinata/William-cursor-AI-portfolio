When you think of connection, you think of the emotional.

But it’s also physical.

In my conversation with Charles Duhigg he explained something fascinating from neuroscience. 

When you’re in a meaningful conversation:
 • Heart rates begin to match
 • Breathing patterns sync
 • Even pupil dilation becomes similar
 • Brain activity starts aligning

This phenomenon is called neural entrainment. It’s one of the core reasons we feel connected to someone.

If you lead a team, negotiate, sell, or manage people, this insight changes how you approach conversations. Presence isn’t just what you say. It’s about how aligned you become with the person across from you.
