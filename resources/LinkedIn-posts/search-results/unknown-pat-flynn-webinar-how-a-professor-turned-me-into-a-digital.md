How a professor turned me into a digital marketer
The professor’s question was straight forward: can you teach me digital marketing? We’d met about 6 years earlier at a conference, and I’d given him my book, Bridges to the Customer’s Heart.

I never heard from him again after that.

A one-time VC (Vice Chancellor), he held top positions in international organizations across the world.

Out of the blues he called to ask if I could teach him digital marketing.

I was taken aback because as at that time I didn’t even know how to join a Zoom meeting.

I told the prof. I didn’t know anything about digital marketing so I couldn’t teach it.

The prof. reminded me of my blog posts and WhatsApp broadcast messages.

I’d been sharing what I’d been learning from tens of gurus including:
1.    Pat Flynn
2.    Joe Polish
3.    Tim Ferriss
4.    Ramit Sethi
5.    Stu Mclaren
6.    Jay Abraham
7.    Marie Forleo
8.    Amy Porterfield
9.    James Althucher
10. Brendon Burchard

The prof. hinted he thoroughly liked what I was sharing and believed I could teach digital marketing.

If you’re familiar with the concept of 10% Edge (though I didn’t know it then), that was it.

I first heard about the 10% Edge from Danny Iny, the training guru, based in Canada. 

Long story short, I asked the prof. whether he would buy a course if I designed one.

I knew nothing about a seminar funnel then as taught by Russell Brunson.

I knew nothing about a lead magnet, landing page and call to action (CTA).

Luckily I knew how to create a slide deck using PowerPoint slides.

At my first webinar (webinar funnel) circa June 2020, I made a CTA: Prof was the first to respond.

He transferred the course fee into my bank account immediately I made the CTA.

That’s how iBMC (Internet Business Mastery Course) began.

The prof. has since gone on to author books and now speaks globally on the world stages.

Not only that, prof. consults as a thought leader and is an African top voice in tertiary education worldwide.

As Danny Iny says, Teach Your Gift.

I wouldn’t have begun teaching and creating courses if the prof. had not challenged me.

The prof. believed in me more that I believed in myself.

Here is the reality: you don’t need to know 100% more than someone else.

All you need is to know 10% more – that’s your 10% Edge.

Today iBMC is rated a top course in digital marketing with 12 cohorts sold out, with candidates from Nigeria, Ghana and USA.

How has someone made you to believe in yourself?
