5 experts. 5 men. 0 women. Happy International Women's Day 🙃

I saw this post about an AI Super Team: 5 Claude Skills modeled after marketing and business experts and their frameworks.

Hormozi. Ogilvy. Gary Vee. Brunson. Suby.

All men. 

First off, there's a whole separate conversation about whether it's ethical to "clone" someone's thinking into an AI persona without their involvement. That's worth examining.

But if people are going to do this (and clearly 1,700+ commenters are lining up for it), the least they could do is NOT build an all-male roster and call it the ultimate team.

Because women have built equally rigorous, equally actionable frameworks across every one of these domains too.

So here are 5 women whose work deserves the same recognition, matched role for role:

📐 April Dunford → The Positioning Architect
Her 5-component positioning framework from "Obviously Awesome" is the gold standard for product-market fit. She just released a 2nd edition in 2026.

✍️ Alex Cattoni → The Copy Chief
Named Marketer of the Year by DigitalMarketer, voted #1 most popular copywriter in 2023. She teaches direct response copywriting and persuasion with proven formulas, the modern, teachable version of what Ogilvy pioneered.

📱 Jasmine Star → The Content Strategist
Law school dropout turned 8-figure entrepreneur and SaaS founder. She teaches social-first content strategy and repurposing with more systems thinking than "just post more."

🎯 Amy Porterfield → The Funnel Architect
Her Digital Course Academy has generated $60M+ in revenue. She teaches webinar funnels, list-building, and launch frameworks that directly parallel Brunson's work. NYT bestselling author, actively coaching and podcasting in 2026.

📣 Bozoma Saint John → The Brand & Growth Strategist
Ghanaian-American, Hall of Fame inducted, Forbes’ #1 Most Influential CMO. She’s held C-suite marketing roles at Netflix, Apple, Uber, and PepsiCo, scaling customer acquisition at the biggest brands on the planet. 

I'm not saying don't learn from the men on the original list. But when we only ever elevate male "experts" as the canon of business thinking, we erase the women doing equally important work.

And if we're going to use AI to replicate expert thinking, maybe the frameworks we study should reflect the full range of people who built them.

Happy International Women's Day! 💪

Who else would you add?
