At the Gold Circle, I had the chance to meet with Jon Penberthy. If you don’t know Jon—he’s the mind behind AdClients and AdCon, and has helped businesses generate over $130M through his funnel strategies.

What hit me wasn’t the big numbers. It was the way he framed business growth. Straightforward. Human. Founder-to-founder.

Here are some of the notes I took down almost word for word from Jon:
👉 “Your funnel is not about you. It’s about them.”
 Stop building complicated pathways to prove you’re clever. Build simple systems that guide people where they want to go.
👉 “You don’t need a marathon webinar. You need 30 minutes of real value.”
 Jon’s team uses short, punchy funnels that give people a win right there and then. Proof first. Pitch later.
👉 “Trust is your real conversion metric.”
 Sales don’t come because you pushed harder. They come because you built trust before you asked.
👉 “Momentum comes from simplicity.”
 If your system feels heavy, it’s wrong. Simplicity creates speed. And speed creates growth.

Those four lines alone were worth the whole session.

My takeaway:
As founders, we often overcomplicate things because we want to look bigger, smarter, or more ready than we are. Jon reminded me: the most profitable path is usually the simplest one.

So this month, I’m asking myself — where can I make the path clearer, lighter, and faster for the people I want to serve?

What about you?
