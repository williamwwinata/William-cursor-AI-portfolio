It's about that time of year when your feed starts to showing conferences and webinars from American gurus.

You know the ones.

Ten years ago, they were social media experts, then three years ago they became crypto experts and now, they're apparently AI thought leaders.

They might be running a free webinar (with endless emails trying to upsell you) or perhaps they are even coming to Australia to be a keynote speaker at some big soiree in Sydney.

Here's my memory of something Gary Vaynerchuk himself said in a podcast episode about five years ago.

"Why the hell would you pay $3000 to see me speak at some conference full of suit-wearing stiffs when everything I believe, everything I do, everything I teach and everything I am has been posted on LinkedIn and YouTube and TikTok and even Facebook since I started doing this stuff?"

And he's right.

There's nothing that Chris Do or Codie Sanchez or Tony Robbiins or Brene Brown will teach you at a conference that they haven't already put in their books, their YouTube videos or their posts on LinkedIn.

You won't learn anything more than you already can from what they've already put online.

Even if you are overwhelmed at the idea of digging through all their content to find the gold, don't.

Just tell an AI to find it.

I did exactly this with Agent Mode on ChatGPT and it not only found everything I could possibly want to know about the teachings of Alex and Leila Hormozi, it organised it into topics, and then laid out an interactive online course that I could publish on to my own web server and work my way through over 90 days.

And all this was done in the same free version of ChatGPT you're probably using.

$16,000 to fly to the US to attend a Hormozi mastermind? Nah, I'll just make my own and do it from my own couch in Darwin, thanks.

Don't fall for the trap in thinking that you'll only learn from the masters if you are in the same room as them along with 1500 other people, seated in a cheaper seat around 50 metres back from the stage.

You won't.
