𝗧𝗵𝗲 "𝗔𝘄𝗸𝘄𝗮𝗿𝗱 𝗣𝗶𝘃𝗼𝘁" 𝗸𝗶𝗹𝗹𝘀 𝘆𝗼𝘂𝗿 𝘀𝗮𝗹𝗲𝘀. 📉

You know the moment. You spend 45 minutes teaching great content. The energy runs high. Then... you "transition" to the pitch.

Your voice changes. You sweat. The audience feels the shift. You sound like a used car salesman.

How do you fix it? Pitch early.

I learned this from Pat Flynn and the Deep Pocket Monster channel: State your offer in the first 10 minutes.

"Hey, I'm going to teach you X, Y, and Z today. You can do this on your own. But at the end, I'm going to show you how I can walk alongside you to get it done faster."

Then you go back to teaching.

When you reach the end, you're not making a "pitch." You're delivering the logical conclusion to the story you've been telling. You're offering them a "Magic Sword" to slay their dragon.
