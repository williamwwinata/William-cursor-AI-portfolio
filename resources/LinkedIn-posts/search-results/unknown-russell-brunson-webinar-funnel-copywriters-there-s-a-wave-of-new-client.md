Copywriters, there's a wave of new clients incoming.

Here's how to capitalize on it.

Russell Brunson just announced the revival of Funnel Hacking Live and a major push for ClickFunnels.

If that doesn't mean anything to you, let me explain why it should.

Back in 2016-2018, Brunson trained thousands of coaches, consultants, and experts to build online businesses. Those people needed funnels. They needed emails. They needed sales pages, webinar scripts, and ad copy.

They needed copywriters.

I built a significant chunk of my career off that wave — by learning exactly what he was teaching his students, identifying the gaps in their execution, and showing up with solutions they already knew they needed.

The approach was simple:

Find businesses that had completed Brunson's training (the 2 Comma Club list was public), study the model they were taught, identify what they were missing or struggling with, then reach out with specific ideas — not generic "I'm a copywriter, need help?" messages.

That last part matters. 

When you understand what someone was taught, you can see where they'll get stuck. And when you show up already speaking their language with ideas tailored to their situation, you skip the "convince them to hire a copywriter" conversation entirely.

You go straight to "when do we start?"

That wave is about to happen again.

Brunson and his team are about to bring a flood of new entrepreneurs into the online business world. These people will be building funnels, launching courses, selling coaching, and running ads — most of them for the first time.

They're going to need help. 

Here's how to get ahead of it:

Go through Brunson's free content now. Expert Secrets and DotCom Secrets are both available for free. Understand the frameworks he teaches so you can speak the language his students will be using.

Pay attention to the gaps. His training covers strategy and structure. What it doesn't cover in depth is the copywriting that makes the funnels actually convert. That's where you come in.

Start building proof now. Personal proof. Build your own funnel. Run your own emails. Test your own ads. 

When you sit across from a potential client and say "here's what I built and here's what it did," the conversation changes completely.

Position yourself as the person who understands their world — the specialist who already knows the model they're running and can make it perform better.

The window on waves like this is finite. Brunson will push hard, 1000's of new businesses will launch, they'll need help, and then the market will stabilize again. The people who get in position now get to eat. 

"By the time you see the bandwagon, it's too late to hop on."

This is what we teach inside The Copywriter's Path — how to spot these opportunities, build proof through your own projects, and show up to client conversations with something better than a cold pitch template and a prayer.
