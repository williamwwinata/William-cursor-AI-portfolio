There's so much information overload going on and one of the best ways to protect your mental health today is by refusing to become a dumpster. 

We’re constantly being bombarded with notifications, messages, voice notes, articles, to-do lists, tasks that don’t concern us, and let’s not even talk about the endless webinars and WhatsApp groups.

But here’s the truth: 
Not all information is useful to you currently. Not all knowledge is necessary, at least not for now.

Pat Flynn in his book, 'Lean Learning' recommends how to learn just enough to solve the current problem, enough to level up the skills we’re actively using, enough to stay relevant without getting overwhelmed.
And as a multi-influential building Heels and Tech, amongst many other things, this is golden. 

Just-in-time learning trumps just-in-case learning. 

You don't need to hoard information for a future that may never come. Instead, be strategic, be present and be selective.

Find ways to:

1. Audit your info sources and mute groups that don’t serve your goals. Unsubscribe from newsletters you never read. Say no to things that don’t align.

2. Also switch from consumer to curator: Don’t consume everything screaming for your attention online. Learn to sieve what you give your attention. 
Ask yourself: 
Is this helpful now? 
Is this adding value to what I’m building today?

3. Additionally, learn to block off learning sprints and focus on one topic at a time. Multitasking is no longer an admirable skill in this 21st century. 
If you’re improving your presentation skills, don't get distracted by UI/UX tutorials. 
Stay in your lane till you master it.

4. Learn to also build before you binge. Instead of always amassing information, rather metabolize. 
Learn, apply, build something small, relearn and keep the loop going. Feedback will teach you faster than theory ever could.

5. Protect your head space. 
Not all conversations, tasks, and trends deserve your attention and input. Learn to detect distractions dressed in the suit of relevance and cut the off with brutal focus.

Use it to declutter your information diet and start out your Monday on a fresh note.
