Last week Alex Hormozi sold over 3.1 million book copies in a webinar.

(experts are saying that’ll net him something like $150M+)

Naturally, people rushed to dissect the magic funnel he used…
The offer…
The upsells…
The backend…
The sequencing…

But here’s the thing, I believe they all missed the real story.

The funnel wasn't magic. The tactics weren't revolutionary. What created that nine-figure payday was something far more powerful than any marketing mechanic could deliver…

Credibility.

Don’t get me wrong. Hormozi's execution was flawless, his strategy brilliant, his presentation masterful. But here's what the ‘tactics’ obsessives ignore: by the time viewers clicked play on that webinar, they were already pre-sold on the man behind the message.

Think about it:

✨ He brought years of in-the-trenches business experience online
✨ Positioned himself as the “go-to guy” for the entrepreneurship world
✨ Engineered omnipresence until people couldn’t ignore him (whether you liked him or not, you knew his name)
✨ Controlled the narrative so people saw him as a leader
✨ Stacked proof signals until skepticism had no room

This was a deliberate campaign to become so visible, so valuable, that when he said, “Here’s my book.” … people didn’t just buy a book.

They bought into him.

This changes everything.

While some are chasing the hacks and split-testing headlines, the real winners understand a deeper truth. Authority trumps tactics. Trust beats tricks. Credibility converts better than any megalomaniac funnel.

So what I got from this is, don’t just study his funnels.

Study the years he spent building authority before ever selling 3 million books.

That’s the piece everyone’s overlooking.

Not the sale… the setup.
