Most of the time you repurpose content to market your business on the socials.
But what if you repurposed your content to monetize it?

You’ve already done the hard part (which is creating valuable content).

Now it’s about finding the version of that content people would happily pay for.

A few ideas and 3 real world examples:

⟫ Blog post → Paid template:
If your blog post gets consistent traffic, turn it into a plug-and-play template.
You already taught the “how.” Now give them the tool to do it faster.

⟫ Carousels → Action guide:
Take your 10 best-performing educational posts and turn them into a step-by-step Google Doc.
Add explanations and examples. Make it step-by-step and take them a few levels deeper. 

⟫ Webinar → Email course + template:
If your webinar was full of gold, break it into digestible daily emails and include a companion worksheet so people can take action. Add additional examples, resources, and actionable steps to get to the results faster.

⟫ High-performing Instagram hooks → Behind-the-scenes doc:
Show what worked, why it worked, and the results. 
Sell it as a “creator’s swipe file” with context.

⟫ Launch results → Behind-the-scenes doc:
Mariah Coz created the Launch Debrief Collection which gives you behind-the-scenes details and data from her 6 & 7 figure launches. 
Sometimes seeing what worked and what didn't work can be invaluable for your customers.

⟫ Business book notes → Key takeaways doc:
I've also seen creator Deya A. turn her notes from reading business books into a Business book club notion database. It has her personal key takeaways from books she has read, with exercises and examples to help her customer take action quickly. 

⟫ My client timeline tracker → make it available to clients:
I have a client timeline tracker that I use for myself. 
It helps you see:
 → your client load for the year
 → if you're over extended
 → what spots you still need to book to hit revenue goals
 → what your monthly/ annual income will be
 → when contracts will end
 → when you need to reduce workload for vacay, and more. 

And it doesn't even take up a whole page in Google sheets. 
Simple, quick, and effective.
I made it available to my mentoring clients and it's become the one spreadsheet they still tell me is their favourite. 

You’re sitting on digital product potential.
Not everything has to be built from scratch.

Sometimes your next scalable offer is already written and hiding in your Google docs somewhere.

It just needs a new container.

What content could you turn into a product this month?
