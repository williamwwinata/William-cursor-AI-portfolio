How do you make $105M in a weekend?


On August 17, 2025, Alex Hormozi did just that and broke the Guinness World Record for the fastest-selling non-fiction book with $100M Money Models.

Beyond the headline, the launch was a masterclass in how business success is engineered. 

Here are the lessons I’m taking away (thanks to Tom Bilyeu's post)

1. Make money fun by believing in what you sell
During his 30+ hour livestream, Alex wasn’t pushing or begging — he was genuinely enjoying himself. Because he believed in the book he’d written over years, selling felt less like selling and more like serving his audience.

2. Trust is the ultimate currency
Half a million people opted in because Alex has been giving free value for years. People buy more of what you’ve already given them.

3. Small thinking = small results
Where most do 1-hour webinars, he ran 30+ hours across 3 days. He aimed for nine figures, not six. Perfectly personifying "Shoot for the stars to land on the moon"

4. Success is engineered
This wasn’t luck. Guinness judges were on-stream. Every detail of the launch proved his method — and became a case study in itself. Show don't tell.

5. Networks are unfair advantages
Alex has one of the strongest networks out there — not by accident, but because he built relationships long before he needed anything. That’s when they matter most.

Thank you Alex Hormozi for shattering limiting beliefs about what’s possible.
