I just reverse-engineered a sales letter from Jason Fladlien (the guy who's done $250M+ in webinar sales).

It's for his AI webinar tool "Project Mona Lisa" and the structure is BRILLIANT.

Here are the 12 elements he uses to turn skeptics into buyers:

1. THE AUTHORITY DISMISSAL OPENER
"None of my accolades will matter a few months from now..."
→ He dismisses his credentials to create intrigue (then brings them back later with proof)

2. THE SHARED ENEMY
"AI could do my job, for me..."
→ Names the fear everyone has, then becomes the guide who solved it first

3. THE MECHANISM REVEAL
Not a prompt pack. Not a course. A plug-and-play AI software with his knowledge CODIFIED.
→ Clear differentiation from commoditized solutions

4. THE SOCIAL PROOF STACK
$9.8M launch. $57.9M launch. $105M with Hormozi. Name-drops: Tony Robbins, Russell Brunson.
→ Specific numbers + recognizable names = credibility

5. THE BETA EXCLUSIVITY PLAY
"We're only letting a small group of savvy entrepreneurs in..."
→ Scarcity + selectivity = increased perceived value

6. THE THREE-PATH COMPARISON
Path 1: DIY (80+ hours, gambling)
Path 2: Hire someone ($5K-$50K, still gambling)
Path 3: His solution ($997, proven frameworks)
→ Makes the decision feel obvious

7. THE FEATURE → BENEFIT TRANSLATION
Each tool solves a specific pain:

Buyer Profile Generator → Know exactly what to say
Script Writer → Customized to YOUR offer
Objection Smasher → Handle tough questions like a pro

8. THE TRANSPARENT OBSTACLE
"We're NOT offering refunds... and here's why"
→ Turns potential objection into a qualifier (only serious people need apply)

9. THE COST REFRAME
Not $997 for software. It's $997 vs. $5K/hour consulting or $35K/day strategy sessions.
→ Anchoring makes the price feel like a steal

10. THE FAQ AS OBJECTION HANDLER
Addresses every possible hesitation:

Tech skills needed? Nope
Works in my niche? Yes
Never done webinars? Even better
→ Removes friction before checkout

11. THE FUTURE PRICE THREAT
"This will NEVER be this cheap again... 4-5 days only"
→ Urgency + regret minimization

12. THE BINARY CHOICE CLOSE
"You either get this now... or you keep gambling with copywriters and ad spend"
→ Forces decision, frames inaction as the real risk

The formula in one sentence:

Authority + Shared Problem + Unique Mechanism + Proof + Exclusivity + Clear Path + Urgency = High-Converting Sales Letter
