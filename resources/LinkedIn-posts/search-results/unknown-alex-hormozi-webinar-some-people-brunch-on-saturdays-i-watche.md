Some people brunch on Saturdays.
I watched Alex Hormozi nearly solve world hunger.


I spent over 5 hours on Saturday watching him launch a book.

But let’s be honest, it wasn’t just a launch.

It was like Shark Tank met QVC and had an MBA baby.

Somewhere in there, he broke a world record…
and still dropped freebies like they were party favors.

 Stacks of playbooks.
 Virtual webinar tickets flying.
 His own AI masterpiece running in the background.
 And grown men in green Gumby suits hustling as stagehands.

It was chaotic. 
Brilliant. 
Unforgettable.

If you stuck it out until the end, you caught the best part (IMO): 
entrepreneurs breaking down their own strategies and pricing models live.

👉 Takeaway #1:
Branding comes first. Before the offer. Before the pricing model.

👉 Takeaway #2:
If your offers aren’t converting, one slight shift can turn them from ugly to irresistible.

👉 Takeaway #3:
Behind every buff dude is a freaking smart lady. 
Did you catch Leila Hormozi's segment?

And just when I thought it was over, 
oh surprise!
We're live on Sunday too.

Not a replay.
He did the entire thing again.

Pulling something like this off takes strategic planning and loads of data.

So don’t fall into the trap of comparing yourself to Alex Hormozi.
Instead, take what you can from the book and online teachings.

And most importantly, implement something. TAKE ACTION
Don’t just put it on the shelf with all your other binders and stacks of notes. 

📌 So tell me, did you walk away with takeaways or 200 books? 

And jokes aside... what Alex and Leila pulled off this weekend was nothing short of impressive. 👏
