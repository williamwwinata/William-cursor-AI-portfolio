I planned my outfit for this talk down to the last detail. 

Pinstripe jacket on pinstripe vest (yep it works!). My favourite pair of pants that I bought in Japan. Comfy but cute shoes. And carefully chosen accessories, earrings and a heart shaped brooch pinned to my jacket.

But when it was time to get mic’ed up, with one of those Madonna mics that wrap around your ear, the AV tech said the earrings had to come off. 

As much as that would have annoyed me in the past, (I mean I NEVER leave the house without a pair of earrings), I paused, took them off, and thought, no worries. I still have the brooch.

How meta is this? The talk I was about to give is called, “The Dollars are in the Details.”

And a detail I'd planned saved me when another detail got taken away.

Maybe it’s a coincidence, but it has become a habit. Over the past 15 years that Omar Zenhom and I have been building our businesses, it's been the quiet little details we focused on (that others phoned in) that gave us our edge. Not a hack or some big strategy. 

Things like the quality of our audio, making sure every podcast episode delivered more value than the previous one, the care in our design, and staying obsessively close to our customers. Interviewing them, running webinars with them, tracking every single piece of feedback they gave us.

When people say don't sweat the small stuff, I think they're missing it. 

Slowing down, paying attention, caring about the details, I believe that's what makes you stand out.   It's not the most popular message. But it's the one that's worked best for us, every single time.

What's a small detail in your business or your life, that ended up mattering more than you expected?
