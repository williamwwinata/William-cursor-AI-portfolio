This truth hits hard for serious brands.

Not long ago, I was listening to a podcast by one of the best webinar guys right now — Jason Fladlien

He talked about how fast they run out of copy for almost every campaign they run.

At first, that surprised me.

But when I sat with it — especially as someone currently writing copy for multiple offers — it made complete sense.

Listen.

Doing things “right” isn’t enough if everything you’re doing isn’t pointing in one direction.

Traffic alone doesn’t win.

Content alone doesn’t win.

Engagement alone doesn’t win.

You need to direct attention.

Every post.

Every email.

Every page.

Toward a specific action.

Is it:

Sign up?

Book a call?

Buy now?

Request a quote?

Here’s what you should never forget.

The more actions you want your prospects to take, the more copy you need.

Because people don’t move on command.

They move when they clearly see what changes after they act.

Your job is to keep showing them:

what life looks like before

what life looks like after

and why now makes sense

From different angles.

Over and over.

Without assuming they “already get it.”

That’s why campaigns burn through copy fast.

And that’s normal.
