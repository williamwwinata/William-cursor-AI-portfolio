Stop teaching so much in sales.

Do this instead to close more deals this month:

Most founders think more value means more steps.

More slides.  
More tactics.  
More how-to.

Then they wonder why no one buys.

I learned this the hard way.

Early in my career, I ran webinars packed with detail.

I showed people everything.

• Which buttons to click  
• Which settings to choose  
• Which scripts to use  

People thanked me.

Then they left and tried to do it themselves.

Sales stayed flat.

The shift came when I stopped teaching execution.

And started teaching perspective.

Alex Hormozi shared a better way.

Instead of:
“Open Ads Manager → set budget to $50 → choose this targeting…”

Try:
“Most ads fail from weak hooks and poor testing. Not from technical setup. Here’s proof. Before. After. See the shift?”

No overwhelm.  
No checklist.  
No step-by-step.

So focus on:

• What’s possible  
• Why it works  
• What changes when you apply it  

Here’s why this works.

Step-by-step advice fits a small group.

Only those ready.  
Only those with the right setup.  
Only those able to act now.

Everyone else tunes out.

But when you shift beliefs, you reach the whole room.

You change how they see the problem.

And when people see the problem in a new way, they buy implementation.

If your pitches are not converting, try this:

Remove half the how-to.

Add proof.  
Add context.  
Add clarity on why.

Sell what to do.

Sell the outcome.

Keep the how for paying clients.

You will close more deals.

And your audience will value you more for it.

Teach less.

Sell more.
