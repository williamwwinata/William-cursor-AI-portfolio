Back a few years ago…

My business partner Will tells me that he booked an important meeting and I needed to be present

He told me it wasn’t for me, but for him.

And all I needed to do was to show up and listen…

So I show up thinking I'm doing him a favor…

And I walk into a room with TWO people.

Will… and some guy I'd never met before.

Turns out this guy had a crypto program he wanted to launch.

A webinar teaching people a new way to make money with crypto.

The funny part is…

Will had already heard the pitch!!!

This guy had presented the idea to Will first…

And Will knew EXACTLY how I'd react if he tried pitching it to me over the phone.

I would've said no before he even finished the sentence.

Because I'd already seen versions of this. And watched them fail.

My brain would've auto-rejected the idea in about 30 seconds flat.

"Yeah, crypto webinar. Heard it. Doesn't work. Next."

But Will KNEW that about me.

He knew my experience would get in the way.

So he baited me into the meeting. Got me in the room. Face to face with the guy who came up with the idea.

And once I was actually sitting there… hearing it live… asking real questions  

I started seeing what they saw.

We ended up structuring a small beta.

18 people each one payed $10K each. 

We had full transparency and EVEN documented everything.

That foundation became the blueprint for the full launch.

226 days later, The Plan hit $57.9 million.

So here's the lesson

The more experience you have, the more dangerous your automatic "no's" become.

Most days they protect you.

But every now and then… they quietly kill something massive before it even gets a chance.

That's why the right partners matter.

Not the ones who always agree with you.

The ones who won’t let you down, even if that means tricking you…

If I'd trusted my gut alone that day… I would've walked away feeling smart.

And I would've been VERY, VERY wrong
