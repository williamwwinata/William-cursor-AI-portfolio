Alex Hormozi pushes out content like a machine.
Instead of creating more,
he’s distributing better.

The core system is simple:
- Create a single long-form piece of content (podcast, YouTube, keynote).
- Slice it into 10–20 smaller pieces (short clips, tweets, carousels).
- Distribute everywhere (LinkedIn, X, TikTok, Insta, email).
- Repeat consistently → attention compounds.

For small businesses, here’s what you can actually steal (without a team of 20 editors):

🔹 Start with one “pillar” format.
Your blog post, a webinar recording, even a LinkedIn post that did well.

🔹 Break it down.
Turn that into 5 LinkedIn posts, 3 tweets, and 1 email to your list.

🔹 Automate lightly.
Tools like Taplio, Repurpose.io, or even ChatGPT can repurpose formats for you.

🔹 Stay consistent.
One strong idea can travel a dozen times if you package it right.

Most founders think they need new ideas every day.
But distribution beats novelty.

Small shifts (like reusing your best ideas in multiple places) can triple your reach without triple the workload.
