As an entrepreneur, I used to focus heavily on funnel optimization. Higher click-thru rate, lower cost per acquisition, etc.

…instead of asking whether the actual offering was truly valuable.

I always thought that success and breakthrough were right around the corner- if only I can get my cost per webinar registration from $12 to $8, etc. etc… only to have customers churn out after a few months anyways.

I used to look up to people like Russel Brunson, Tony Robbins, Dean Graziosi, Dan Lok, etc.

…Now I look down on them.

Now, I spend most of my time improving my product itself (which is entirely different- actual physical products rather than coaching). I am always asking and testing whether people truly want what I have to offer. 

You’re never “one funnel away”, despite what industry gurus want you to believe. The entire notion of funnels is dangerous because it’s myopic, one-track thinking. 

If you want big success and not short-lived flash, then:

Your product must genuinely improve people’s lives and generally be awesome. If you can’t pull that off, no amount of marketing, flash, or promotion can save you from eventual ruin of your offer. Then you’ll be left having to create another gimmick, and another, and another.

Take the time to really consider what people want. Then, take the time to serve the market really, really well. 

If it’s a gimmick, it’s gonna die.

If it requires high pressure sales, it’s gonna die.

Just my two cents.
