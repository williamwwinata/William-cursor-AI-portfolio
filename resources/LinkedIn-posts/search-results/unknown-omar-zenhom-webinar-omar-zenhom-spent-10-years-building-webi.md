Omar Zenhom spent 10 years building WebinarNinja from a landing page and 50 email addresses to 30,000 paid users, 3 million webinar attendees, and a successful acquisition.

At MicroConf Europe 2024 in Dubrovnik, he shared the 10 lessons from that journey publicly for the first time:

→ Make it personal. Your "why" is what gets you out of bed when things get brutal. Find it before you need it.

→ Embrace constraints. Omar ran a team of 30 competing against companies with 1,000 employees. His constraint — being a former teacher, not an engineer — became his edge. He used webinars to communicate directly with customers while his competitors were still scheduling focus group committees.

→ Hire slowly, fire quickly. A bad hire cost Omar $500K. His rule: whoever hired someone must be the one to fire them — so they feel the weight of it and hire more carefully next time.

→ Evolve or get crushed. In 2014 WebinarNinja only did live webinars. By listening to customers every single week, they became one of the first platforms to launch automated webinars and paid webinars. The product that got you here won't get you there.

→ No one does it alone. Solopreneurship isn't a badge of honor — it's doing business on hard mode. Build a team, find your community, and get around people who are a few steps ahead.

→ Cash flow is oxygen. Five out of six businesses fail because of cash flow. Omar looked at his P&L every single day. If you don't know your numbers, you can't make decisions.

→ Choose your sacrifices. You can have it all — just not all at once. Omar and his co-founder Nicole sat down early and decided what mattered: the business, family, and health. Everything else got cut. Communicate it to the people around you before they resent you for it.

→ Know when to sell. The honest answer: Omar knew it was time when he could admit he couldn't take the business further himself. Keeping his heels dug in would have been a disservice to the product and the customers.

→ You are not your business. After 10 years as the face of WebinarNinja, that identity disappeared overnight. What didn't transfer in the sale: the experience and the person he became. That goes with you.

→ Every day in business is a gift. If you're transacting, you're winning. Most people never make it this far.
