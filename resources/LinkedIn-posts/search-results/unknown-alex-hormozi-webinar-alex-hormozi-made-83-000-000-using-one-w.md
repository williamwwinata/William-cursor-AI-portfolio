Alex Hormozi made ~$83,000,000 using one webinar a few days ago.

Let’s break down his funnel and how you can apply it to your business:

→ This is the most aggressive funnel we’ve seen all year.
→ It was direct response copywriting mastery.
→ It was chaotic, brilliant, and unapologetically effective.

Here’s the anatomy of a $100M launch:

1. 4-Phase Email Funnel
▸ Pre-registration
▸ Pre-event reminders
▸ Live “we’re on now!” pings
▸ Post-event “buy the thing” follow-ups
▸ Total emails sent? 23+
▸ Result: Full inbox domination.

2. Scarcity & Urgency, done right
“No idea what it is - but it’s better than an NFT, less than a Bitcoin.”
↳ He teased a free gift only for live attendees.
↳ And then added 3 extra days of live-only bonuses.

3. Free vs Paid Psychology
▸ The book was $40.
▸ But the bonuses?
▸ “Most Incredible Free Gifts Ever” (MIFGEs) worth $35,000+.
▸ He barely talked about the book - he sold the bonuses.

4. Affiliate Program on Steroids
▸ Prizes, leaderboards, ceremonies.
▸ He even mailed letters to top referers.
▸ Physical letters. In 2025.
▸ Insane? Yes. Effective? Also yes.

5. Simple Pages > Pretty Pages
▸ Ugly, copy-heavy pages with endless scrolling.
▸ But optimized for conversion, not aesthetics.
▸ Every scroll = more proof. Every section = new CTA.

6. Live, raw, real-time selling
▸ Hermozi didn’t present.
▸ He read a letter on camera.
▸ He drank a beer out of a shoe.
▸ He ran a 3-day marathon of value + sales, and closed like a boss.

7. 1000+ Ads. Before AND after.
▸ He wasn’t just selling during the stream.
▸ He sold before. He sold after.
▸ He sold everywhere.
▸ And it worked.

8. AI? Yeah, but physical still wins.
▸ Printed playbooks.
▸ Physical binders.
▸ “No digital versions.”
▸ Smart move in a saturated AI world.

Most people overthink funnels.
They’re waiting for perfect design.
Perfect timing.
Perfect automation.

But what they really need?

→ A damn good offer.
→ A compelling story.
→ And the guts to actually sell.

Alex Hermozi went full Russell Brunson this year.

And walked away with $100M.
