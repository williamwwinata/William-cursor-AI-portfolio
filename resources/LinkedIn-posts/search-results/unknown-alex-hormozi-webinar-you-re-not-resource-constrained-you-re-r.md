You’re not resource-constrained. You’re resource-misaligned.

Most leaders hear “resources” and think money.

That’s the smallest part of the equation.

I shared my learnings in a webinar in December of last year from a workshop I attended with Alex Hormozi.

This was one of the key takeaways I gained from the workshop.

Most leaders are laser-focused on money. 

But the teams that burn out, miss deadlines, and lose momentum aren't always underfunded.

They're under-resourced in ways the numbers never show.

There are actually 5 resources every business runs on:

🕧Time: the only one you can never get back
🧑‍💼Attention: fragmented focus kills execution
💰Money: important, but not the whole story
🪫Energy: depleted leaders make expensive decisions
💪Effort: intensity is finite; where it goes matters

🧱You can raise capital and still stall.

🚗You can hire talent and still drift.

🎯You can increase budget and still miss targets.

Because money cannot compensate for scattered attention.

🥱It cannot fix exhausted energy.

It cannot create time that does not exist.

👓Growth happens when all five are aligned around one clear priority.

5️⃣The most intentional leaders we work with plan for all five, not just the one with a dollar sign in front of it.

So before your next planning session, ask:

❓Which of these 5 is quietly draining my team right now?
❓Do we have the time?
❓Do we have the focused attention?
❓Do we have the energy to execute well?
❓Is the effort aligned with our top objective?
❓And yes, do we have the money?

Plan accordingly for you and your colleagues.

⬇️When even one of the five is ignored, performance erodes quietly.

⬆️When all five move in the same direction, momentum compounds.
